## The Consequences of Roadcheck 2024 Violations

Now that the 2024 CVSA International Roadcheck inspection blitz has concluded, you may think your commercial vehicles and employees who drive are in the clear from federal interventions.

**If either were cited for violations during the inspection process, this isn't the case.**

Companies with drivers or trucks that were cited for violations or placed out of service during the Roadcheck 2024 inspections will be prioritized for DOT audits during the following months.

**Now is the time to prepare for potential upcoming DOT audits to avoid additional violations and out-of-service orders. Meeting the latest FMCSA regulations and having organized, 100% digital DOT records will increase any company's chances of passing onsite or offsite audits.**

## 1. Create & Maintain Digital DOT Driver Qualification Files

Now that offsite audits are becoming the FMCSA's go-to method of conducting both safety audits and compliance reviews, companies should migrate from paper-based files to a digital DOT filing solution.

The FMCSA now requires companies to submit files electronically during an offsite audit — **and these files must be submitted within 48 hours**. For companies still maintaining paper files, this means they must audit, scan, and upload all requested documents to the auditor within two days.

### The Driver Qualification File must contain the driver's:
- DOT-compliant application
- Motor Vehicle Report
- Certificate of Road Test
- Medical Examination Report
- Medical Examiner's National Registry Verification
- Skill Performance Evaluation Certificate Application (If Required)

### The Safety Performance History must contain the driver's:
- Record of previous employers contacted
- Driver's written authorization to release information

## 2. Ensure Your DOT Drug & Alcohol Testing Program Is Compliant

According to federal law, all CDL drivers considered "safety-sensitive" employees must participate in a DOT-compliant drug and alcohol testing program. This includes drivers who:

- Operate vehicles weighing at least 26,001 pounds
- Transport hazardous materials
- Transport vehicles with at least 16 passengers

### Comprehensive DOT drug and alcohol testing programs must include:
- Pre-employment testing
- Post-accident testing
- Random testing
- Reasonable suspicion testing
- Return-to-duty/follow-up testing

Violations of any of the above, such as positive DOT drug test results or test refusals, must then be reported to the FMCSA Clearinghouse.

## 3. Manage Your FMCSA Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse Queries

Employers are required to check the Clearinghouse once during the pre-employment process and at least once annually for the duration of each CDL driver's employment.

**Running regular limited queries on each of your drivers allows you to get ahead of any drug or alcohol violations reported to the Clearinghouse. If a DOT auditor finds violations, the driver will be pulled from their safety-sensitive duties until they have completed the return-to-duty process.**

### Limited Clearinghouse Queries

Limited queries only show that a violation record exists for a driver — it doesn't show the details of the violation(s).

Employers can use a blanket consent form to run these queries. This consent form can remain in effect for the duration of a driver's employment, allowing them to run limited queries on drivers as often as they'd like.

If a limited query shows that a record exists for a driver, then an employer is required to run a full query within 24 hours. If the driver doesn't consent to the query, they will need to be pulled from safety-sensitive duties.

> **Reminder:** A new Clearinghouse regulation passed on November 18, 2024, will directly impact drivers with prohibited status in the Clearinghouse. Those who still have this status will have their CDLs/CLPs revoked or denied.

## 4. Ensure Drivers Follow FMCSA Hours-of-Service Regulations

Anyone who operates a commercial vehicle in the US must follow hours-of-service (HOS) regulations. A vehicle is a CMV if it meets any of the following:

- Weighs over 10,000 pounds with or without a load
- Transports hazardous materials (Hazmat) requiring a placard
- Transports 16 or more people, including the driver
- Transports nine or more people, including the driver, for compensation

### HOS Regulations Overview

**CDL 14-Hour Rule**
Following a minimum 10-hour break, employees cannot drive more than 14 consecutive hours.

**11-Hour Driving Limit**
Within that 14-hour shift limit, employees cannot drive for more than a total of 11 hours. Once 11 hours pass, drivers must take a 10-hour break.

**60/70-Hour Weekly Limit**
Drivers can no longer drive CMVs once they have worked 60 hours of on-duty time within seven days, or 70 hours within eight days. This weekly limitation resets after a 34-hour restart.

**34-Hour Restart**
Once drivers hit the 60 or 70-hour weekly limit, they must take 34 consecutive hours of off-duty time.

**30-Minute Rest Breaks**
As soon as employees have driven for eight hours, they need to take a 30-minute break.

## 5. Keep Up on Commercial Vehicle Maintenance

The majority of Roadcheck inspections focused on commercial vehicle safety and maintenance. All vehicles in your fleet should have their own unique inspection, maintenance, and repair schedules.

### According to FMCSA regulation code 396.3(a):
- Every company must systematically inspect, repair, and maintain all motor vehicles subject to its control.
- Parts and accessories must be in safe and proper condition at all times.
- Pushout windows, emergency doors, and emergency door marking lights must be inspected at least every 90 days.

### Top 5 Vehicle FMCSA Violations of 2024:
1. Inoperable required lamp
2. Operating a CMV without proof of a periodic inspection
3. No/discharged/unsecured fire extinguisher
4. No/insufficient warning devices
5. Inoperative turn signal

## 6. Run MVR Checks on Drivers Regularly

As a DOT-regulated business, you must pull annual MVRs for every driver you employ to meet federal recordkeeping requirements.

**Running regular MVR checks on your drivers can help avoid surprise traffic or safety violations being revealed during DOT audits.**

### How Does MVR Monitoring Work?

MVR Monitoring keeps tabs on driving records. When there is a change, employers are immediately alerted to any "hit" and receive the updated MVR for review.

These "hits" could be anything from accidents or DUIs to speeding tickets or other violations. They could also signify CDL suspensions or revocations, safety violations, or medical certificate downgrades — as well as positive changes, such as when a driver renews their CDL or medical certificate.
