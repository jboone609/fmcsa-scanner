# Nuclear Verdicts in the Transportation Industry
## A Guide for Fleet Safety, Compliance, and Risk Teams

## A Real-World Example

A Missouri appellate court upheld a **$20 million verdict** against Great Plains Trucking after a fatal accident on Interstate 70 in 2019. The company had an unwritten policy allowing drivers to exceed the speed limit, lacked essential safety guidelines in its CDL manual, and failed to implement corrective measures after the accident. The court found these practices indicative of a broader disregard for safety protocols.

## A Growing Industry Issue

Nuclear verdicts—jury awards typically exceeding $10 million—have skyrocketed in the trucking industry over the last decade:

- A 2021 verdict: **$1 billion** against two trucking companies after a crash near Jacksonville, FL
- A 2024 case: **$462 million** payout to families of two men killed in a Wabash trailer crash
- October 2020: **$411 million** ordered against one trucking company for a motorcyclist injured in a 45-vehicle pileup

A review of 154 trucking litigation verdicts and settlements from June 2020 to April 2023 revealed:
- Statistical mean plaintiffs' award: **$27.5 million**
- Statistical median award: **$759,875** for settlements

This trend is raising prices and limiting access to Truckers Auto Liability and Excess Liability Insurance.

## Creating a Culture of Safety

FMCSA data shows:
- **87%** of crashes result from driver error
- **10%** are due to mechanical issues
- Only **3%** result from environmental factors

Building a culture of safety and compliance is the simplest and least expensive way to protect the future of your company.

## Your 6-Step Nuclear Verdict Protection Plan

### Step 1: Hiring & Background Screening Practices

- Have each new driver complete a DOT-Compliant Application
- Run the following background screens on all new hires:
  - Motor Vehicle Report (in each state they've held a CDL)
  - Criminal Background Check
  - Commercial Driver's License Information System (CDLIS) search
  - Pre-Employment Screening Program (PSP screen)
  - DOT Pre-Employment Clearinghouse Query
  - Previous Employer Verification
  - DOT Pre-Employment Drug Test

The Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse is a central online database storing drivers' non-negative drug and alcohol test results. Employers must check it before hiring and annually thereafter.

### Step 2: Create and Maintain Compliant Driver Files

Non-compliant driver files account for many of the top acute and critical FMCSA violations annually. Create a fool-proof plan for maintaining compliant Driver Qualification Files. To comply with the FMCSA's offsite audit requirement, files should be maintained in a secure, digital format.

### Step 3: Fight Driver Fatigue and Distraction

Driver fatigue and distraction are leading causes of truck accidents. Key steps:
- Ensure drivers keep good logs and follow hours-of-service regulations
- Provide regular driver fatigue training sessions
- Install in-cab cameras to monitor over-the-road habits
- Encourage drivers with sleep apnea or other conditions to get medical help

### Step 4: Monitor Your Drivers' MVRs

FMCSA regulations require annual MVR pulls—but that leaves 364 days where you could be in the dark. An MVR Monitoring program continuously watches each driver's record and alerts you immediately when new information appears (moving violations, CDL suspensions, DUIs, etc.).

Ignorance is not an acceptable excuse under FMCSA regulations, and it will not be accepted as a valid defense in court.

### Step 5: Prioritize Inspections and Maintenance

Approximately 10% of all accidents result from truck maintenance issues. Make pre- and post-trip inspections part of your fleet's daily routine and ensure your shop addresses issues quickly.

### Step 6: Invest in a Fully-Automated Safety & Compliance Solution

A fully-automated solution manages core safety and compliance programs, ensures smart hiring decisions, sets drivers up in required compliance programs, and monitors key deadlines. All documentation stored securely online will be available in the event of an audit or litigation.
