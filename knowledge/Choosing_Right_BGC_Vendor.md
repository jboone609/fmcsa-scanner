## Question #1 — Has your vendor updated your screening packages with modern background checks?

Too often, screening programs are set up and forgotten — they end up on autopilot. Don't view background screenings as an administrative box to check. A quality screening should arm you with insights that help you make better hiring decisions (bad hires can cost your organization thousands of dollars).

**Key Point:** Your vendor should be progressive when it comes to screenings and recommend a program that makes sense for your organization's specific needs — and regularly revisit this program to ensure it is meeting expectations.

## Question #2 — Are you stuck on a customer service merry-go-round?

If you don't have a dedicated account manager, that's a big red flag. Without one, you're just another contract number.

Signs of a poor vendor relationship:
- Attentive during the sales process, unresponsive after signing
- Different customer service reps each time you call, with no continuity
- More time spent re-explaining your situation than getting answers

**Key Point:** You should receive a proactive, daily update on the status of your screens via email so you're not chasing them down.

## Question #3 — When was the last time your vendor called you first?

A quality screening vendor won't simply wait for you to call them with a question or request. They will proactively check in to solicit feedback, make program adjustments, and provide suggestions for improvement — including notifying you about changes in laws.

**Key Point:** When it comes to background screening vendors, think **relational, not transactional.**

---

## Question #4 — Do they notify you when changes occur that affect your disclosures and authorizations?

Most states have different verbiage that must be presented to job applicants when getting their permission for background checks. This verbiage is constantly changing at the state level.

**Key Point:** Do **not** underestimate the risk that results from providing the wrong disclosures and authorizations to job applicants. Your vendor should alert you whenever there's a change at the state or federal level, and their system should dynamically present the right disclosures based upon where the applicant lives.

## Question #5 — Does your background check vendor still use paper?

Paper made sense thirty years ago. It doesn't now. Your background screening vendor should provide solutions that are **100% paper-free**, start to finish. Everything should be mobile-friendly so applicants can authorize and complete the process on their smartphone.

The vendor's platform should be comprehensive — for example, the drug test you order should fully integrate with the background screening process. If your vendor has different systems for different products, that's usually a sign of obsolescence.

**Key Point:** Background screening vendors should be technologically nimble, forward-thinking, and always looking for ways to make the experience better for job applicants.

## Question #6 — Does your vendor understand your organization and your hiring challenges and goals?

Hiring isn't a static process. Goals change, new challenges come up, and your workforce's needs evolve. This isn't a one-and-done discussion — a good vendor will not only revisit these topics regularly, but will also make recommendations based on the outcomes of these conversations.

**Key Point:** Your vendor should actively seek updates and initiate discussions around your hiring goals and challenges.

---

## Question #7 — Does your vendor look at the past (and maybe the present) and that's it?

The past doesn't always show a complete picture. A quality vendor should provide options for:
- **Pre-employment screenings**
- **Post-hire screenings**
- **Ongoing monitoring**

For example, if you only conduct a pre-hire check and skip ongoing monitoring or post-hire drug screening, you could miss important changes in an employee's behavior or circumstances.

**Key Point:** A quality vendor should provide options for pre-employment screenings and post-hire screenings as well as ongoing monitoring.

## Question #8 — Does your vendor have a "one size fits all" approach?

If you're screening all candidates using the same package, you're likely under-screening and/or inappropriately screening. The best background screening vendors build **custom packages** tailored to your business needs and specific job types.

Example: A credit check makes sense for an accountant role, but is unnecessary for a marketing coordinator.

**Key Point:** The best background screening vendors build custom packages only after taking time to understand your company, hiring process, and specific goals and challenges.

## Question #9 — Does your vendor help you manage the adverse action process?

### What is Adverse Action?
An adverse action is a harmful or unfavorable action taken against a person — typically the denial of credit, insurance, employment, promotion, or another benefit due to credit and/or criminal history.

As an employer, you are required by law to:
1. Inform applicants that you will conduct background checks
2. Obtain explicit consent
3. If rejecting an applicant based on a background check, send a **pre-adverse action notice** including a copy of the check and "A Summary of Your Rights Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act"
4. Allow the applicant time (usually five business days) to review and dispute findings
5. Reinvestigate all disputed information promptly
6. Only then send the final adverse action notice

**Key Point:** Having a quality background screening vendor to help you manage this process is essential. The adverse action process should also be fully online, not paper-based.

## Question #10 — Is your vendor PBSA certified?

The **Professional Background Screening Association (PBSA)** is a trade organization founded in 2003 to advance excellence in the screening profession. Their Background Screening Agency Accreditation Program (BSAAP) is a widely recognized seal of approval.

PBSA certification involves thoroughly vetting a screening company's policies and procedures, including data security. It is not automatic — achieving and maintaining it takes time and commitment.

**Key Point:** Working with a PBSA-certified vendor offers peace of mind, knowing an independent industry organization has already evaluated and certified the vendor.
