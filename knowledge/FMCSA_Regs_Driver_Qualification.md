## Commercial Motor Vehicle

A vehicle is classified as a CMV under any of the following criteria:

**01 – Weight Rating Criteria**
A vehicle is classified as a CMV if it has a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) or gross combination weight rating (GCWR), or gross vehicle weight or gross combination weight of 10,001 pounds (4,536 kilograms) or more, whichever is greater.

**02 – Passenger Capacity**
Any vehicle designed or used to transport more than 8 passengers (including the driver) for compensation, or more than 15 passengers (including the driver) and not used for compensation.

**03 – Hazardous Materials**
Any vehicle, regardless of size, used to transport hazardous materials which requires the vehicle to be placarded under the Hazardous Materials Transportation Act.

## What is 49 CFR 391?

**PART 391 — QUALIFICATIONS OF DRIVERS AND LONGER COMBINATION VEHICLE (LCV) DRIVER INSTRUCTORS**

- FMCSA Regulation
- Sets the minimum requirements for people who drive commercial motor vehicles for motor carriers and the motor carriers themselves
- Outlines the responsibilities of the motor carriers employing those drivers
- If someone is a motor carrier and drives for that carrier, they must fulfil both requirements

---

## 391 Applicability

**01 – Basic Definition of a Motor Carrier**
A motor carrier is defined as any person or company engaged in the transportation of goods or passengers on the nation's public roads and highways utilizing Commercial Motor Vehicles.

**02 – Interstate Commerce**
The vehicle is used in interstate commerce, which involves trade, traffic, or transportation in the United States:
- Between a place in a State and a place outside of such State (including a place outside of the United States)
- Between two places in a State through another State or a place outside of the United States
- Between two places in a State as part of trade, traffic, or transportation originating or terminating outside the State or the United States

## General Qualifications and Disqualifications

### Qualifications
- At least 21 years old
- Can read and speak in English proficiently
- Able to safely operate a CMV
- Is physically qualified to drive a CMV
- Has a valid commercial motor vehicle license
- Has successfully completed a road test and been issued a certificate of driver's road test (if does not hold an operator's license for CMVs per § 391.33)

### Disqualifications
- Loss of Driving Privileges
- Criminal Offenses
- Other Offenses
- Violation of Out of Service Order
- Texting or other use of handheld mobile device (391.15(e) and (f))

## Overview of a Driver Qualification File (DQF)

### Ongoing Updates

| Form/Inquiry/Note to Include | Must Retain Document For |
|---|---|
| **Inquiry To State Agencies for Driving Record – Annual** (49 CFR 391.25(a) and (c)) — Motor carriers must contact State agencies annually for an updated copy of each driver's MVR. | 3 years from date of execution |
| **Review of Driving Record – Annual** (49 CFR 391.25(c)(2)) — At least once every 12 months, the carrier must collect a current MVR, review it, and retain a note with the reviewer's name and date. | 3 years from date of execution |
| **Medical Examination Report and Medical Examiner's Certificate** (49 CFR 391.43) — All commercial drivers are required to pass a physical exam at least once every 24 months. | 3 years from date of execution |
| **Employer note verifying that medical examiner is listed on National Registry of Certified Medical Examiners** (Non-CDL: 49 CFR 391.51(b)(9)(i)); CDL: 49 CFR 391.51(b)(9)(ii)) | 3 years from date of execution |

### Initial DQ File Documents

| Form/Inquiry/Note to Include | Must Retain Document For |
|---|---|
| **Driver's Application for Employment** (49 CFR 391.21) | Life of employment + 3 years after termination |
| **Driver's Road Test Certificate or Equivalent** (49 CFR 391.31(e)) | Life of employment + 3 years after termination |
| **Inquiry to Previous Employers: Safety Performance History Records Request** (49 CFR 391.23(a)(1)and(b)) — Must be completed within 30 days of employment start. | Life of employment + 3 years after termination |
| **Safety Performance History Records: Driver Correction or Rebuttal (if applicable)** (49 CFR 391.23(i)(2) and 49 CFR 391.23(j)(3)) | Life of employment + 3 years after termination |
| **Inquiry To State Agencies for 3-Year Driving Record** (49 CFR 391.23(a)(1)and(b)) — Must be made within 30 days of hire. | Life of employment + 3 years after termination |

| **Longer Combination Vehicle (LCV) Driver Training Certificate** (49 CFR 380.401) | Life of employment + 3 years after termination |

## Application for Employment

Every DQ File must have an Application for Employment in order to be compliant.

The Application for Employment must include:

- **Applicant's Personal Information:** Full name, date of birth, and Social Security number; mailing address and three years of residential history.
- **License Information:** Driver's license number, issuing State, and expiration date for each license held in the past three years; types of vehicles operated.
- **Employment History:** Complete employment history for the previous 10 years, including names and addresses of employers, dates of employment, and reasons for leaving.
- **Experience and Qualifications:** Detailed driving experience including types of equipment operated, approximate number of miles driven, and types of cargo transported; any special courses or training relevant to driving.
- **Safety Record:** A list of all motor vehicle accidents (if any) in which the applicant was involved during the past three years, including dates, nature of accidents, and any fatalities or injuries.
- **Traffic Violations:** A record of any traffic violations other than parking during the last three years.
- **Signature and Date:** The application must be signed and dated by the applicant.

## Safety Performance History Investigations

Employers must conduct a Safety Performance History investigation which investigates Employment, Accident, and D&A History. This must be done for each employer where the prospective employee held a Regulated Driving Job in the past 3 years.

> If the driver operates CDL Vehicles, the Clearinghouse Query fulfills the D&A section.

### Employment Verification
- "General Employment Verification" details related to driver's employment (likely: Dates of Employment, Role)

### Accident History
- List of reportable accidents a driver was involved in while employed with that employer

### Drug & Alcohol History — *No Longer Required in Safety Performance History*
- Positive Alcohol Tests
- Positive Drug Tests
- Refusals to Test
- Documentation of Return to Duty Process
- Verification with Driver of Pre-Employment tests failed

## Medical Certification

**01 – Medical Examination Requirement**
- CMV drivers must be physically qualified and must have a valid Medical Examiner's Certificate (Medical Card).
- The examination assesses various health factors including vision, hearing, and overall physical condition.

**02 – Employer's Responsibility to Review**
- Employers must verify and review the Medical Card during the pre-employment screening process.
- This includes ensuring the Medical Card is current and valid for the type of driving the employee will be performing.

**03 – Record Keeping**
- Employers must maintain a copy of the Medical Card in the Driver Qualification File.
- The Medical Card must be retained for as long as the driver is employed and for three years thereafter.

**04 – Medical Examiner Qualifications**
- Medical examinations must be conducted by a medical examiner listed on the FMCSA National Registry of Certified Medical Examiners.

**05 – Regular Updates**
- Employers must ensure that drivers undergo regular medical examinations, at least once every 24 months.
- In cases where a medical condition requires closer monitoring, more frequent examinations may be necessary.

**06 – Driver Notification**
- Drivers must provide their employer with a new Medical Card prior to the expiration of the current one.

## Motor Vehicle Reports

### Initial MVR
- Motor carriers must obtain an initial MVR from every state where the driver held a motor vehicle operator's license or permit during the preceding three years.
- This MVR must be obtained within 30 days of the driver beginning employment.

### Annual MVR
- Motor carriers are required to review the driving record of each driver annually.
- The annual review must include an MVR from every state that issued a license or permit to the driver during the period being reviewed.

---

## Road Test

### Eligibility for Road Test
- Applicants must possess a valid Driver's License for the type of vehicle they will be operating.

### Vehicle for Test
- The road test must be conducted using a vehicle that represents the type of vehicle the applicant will be operating.

### Test Components
1. Pre-trip vehicle inspection
2. Coupling and uncoupling of combination units (if applicable)
3. Placing the vehicle in operation
4. Use of the vehicle's controls and emergency equipment
5. Operating the vehicle in traffic and on the highway
6. Backing and parking the vehicle
7. Adherence to general safety and operational rules

### Examiner Qualifications
- The test must be conducted by a person who is competent to evaluate and determine the applicant's ability to operate the vehicle safely.

### Duration
- The road test must be of sufficient duration to ensure a comprehensive evaluation of the applicant's driving skills.

### Certification of Road Test
- Upon successful completion of the road test, a Certificate of Road Test must be issued.
- The certificate should include the name of the person tested, the license number, and class of the vehicle used for the test, and the name and signature of the examiner.

### Retention of Certificate
- The original or a copy of the certificate must be retained in the driver's qualification file.

---

## Driver Qualification File Retention Requirements

- **Timeframe:** Overall file is retained for as long as the driver is employed and for three years thereafter. Certain documents only need to be retained from three years of initial execution.
- **Confidentiality and Accessibility:** Managed confidentially and made accessible to authorized personnel only.

> A Motor Carrier must create and retain one Driver File for each driver who is available to drive a CMV under their authority.
