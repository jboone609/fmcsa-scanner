## 1. Medical Certification Integration Rule Overview

The FMCSA Medical Examiner's Certification Integration final rule (NRII) covers how commercial drivers' medical certification information is collected, stored, and shared.

Previously, drivers with commercial learner permits (CLPs) or commercial driver's licenses (CDLs) had to provide a paper copy of their medical examiner's certificate (MEC) to their State Driver's Licensing Agency. This certificate — often referred to as a med card or med cert — confirms that they are physically qualified to drive a commercial motor vehicle.

The long-term goal of the rule is to fully digitize and automate the medical qualification and certification process. Under the Integration rule, certified Medical Examiners report DOT physical results to the FMCSA no later than midnight of the next calendar day. The FMCSA then transmits results to State Driver Licensing Agencies (SDLAs), which removes the need for CDL/CLP drivers to submit paper med cards once a state is live.

As of October 2025, all states are required to use this digital flow. However, implementation status and workflows may still vary by state; confirm your state's current process before relying solely on digital transmission.

### States That Have Not Yet Implemented NRII (as of October 2025)

- Alaska
- California
- Florida
- Iowa
- Kentucky
- Louisiana
- New Hampshire
- New Jersey
- New York
- Oklahoma
- Vermont
- Wyoming

### Temporary Waiver (Oct 13, 2025 – Jan 10, 2026)

FMCSA permits CDL/CLP drivers and motor carriers to use a paper MEC as proof of medical certification for up to 60 days after the exam date while NRII/Integration is finalized. Verify MEC posting to the MVR as soon as available.

## 2. What the Integration Rule Means for CDL & Non-CDL Drivers

### CDL Drivers

Depending on the state they're licensed in, CDL drivers may still be asked to provide a physical MEC copy in states that haven't fully digitized intake; check with your SDLA and medical provider (Oct 2025).

Drivers now need to self-certify with their state licensing agency regarding interstate or intrastate operations, and whether they are medically accepted or not. Drivers' CDLs and CLPs will be downgraded if their medical certificates are expired or not renewed within 60 days of the disqualified notice. Label MEC renewals "Due by: mm/dd/yyyy" in your driver qualification file (DQF) reminder system.

### Non-CDL Drivers

Non-CDL (FMCSA-regulated) drivers still receive physical MECs from the Medical Examiner and must submit them to their SDLA. Keep a copy on-person and in the DQF (Oct 2025).

## 3. What the Rule Means for Employers

Employers must run a motor vehicle report that displays updated medical certificate information before putting CDL/CLP drivers back behind the wheel. Since the verification process will be handled through the National Registry and FMCSA directly, they can have peace of mind that the Medical Examiner is registered.

Employers of non-CDL drivers must continue ensuring the copy of the medical certificate is on the driver's person and placed in the DQF. The Medical Examiner registry verification must still be completed for the State Driver's Licensing Agencies.

**Paper medical certificates will still be processed in some states after the implementation date. The goal is for digital medical cards to be processed for CDL drivers.**

### CDL Driver Employer Checklist

1. Run an MVR and confirm MEC status = Valid before dispatch
2. File the digital MEC proof (screenshot or PDF MVR showing med status) in the DQF
3. Verify the examiner appears in NRCME (document the check)

*As of Oct 2025, some states still process paper in edge cases — if so, retain the temporary document until the MVR reflects the MEC.*

### Non-CDL Driver Employer Requirement

Confirm the driver carries the physical MEC, and file a copy plus NRCME verification in the DQF.

> **Note:** Employers must continue checking that their drivers are deemed qualified by a Certified Medical Examiner and include a note in the DQF verifying that the examiner is listed in the National Registry.

## 4. Medical Certificate MVR Requirements

For CDL drivers, the motor vehicle record (MVR) is the system-of-record for medical qualification (Oct 2025). As a DOT-regulated business, you're well-accustomed to the process of pulling annual MVRs for every driver you employ. You must do so to meet federal recordkeeping requirements. But there are also specific MVR requirements for verifying medical certificates.

If the CDLIS MVR contains medical certification status information, employers must obtain the CDLIS motor vehicle record from the current licensing state and place proof of this record in the driver qualification file.

Now that the Integration rule is in place, **employers of CDL drivers cannot place a driver on the road until a valid medical certificate is shown on an MVR.** Because physical medical cards are being eliminated for CDL drivers, running this MVR check will be the only way employers can prove their commercial drivers are medically qualified.

### Always On: Continuous MVR Monitoring

Increasingly, as a safety measure, fleets are leveraging MVR Monitoring — a solution that's continually tapped into the SDLAs to monitor for driver updates 24/7. When a change to a driver's record (also known as a "pointer") is reported from the state to a monitoring software, an MVR check is automatically triggered to run.

**MVR Monitoring alerts employers of:**

- Accidents
- Speeding and other motor vehicle violations
- DUI violations
- CDL suspensions or revocations
- Medical certificate downgrades and renewals

When a record is updated with information that triggers any of these pointers, a complete report will be generated, and you'll be notified as soon as possible.

## 6. Create & Maintain Digital DOT Driver Qualification Files

It is highly recommended that employers regularly check their driver qualification files to make sure there is a valid medical card on file for every driver they employ.

The FMCSA now requires companies to submit files electronically during an offsite audit — **and these files must be submitted within as little as 48 hours.** For companies still maintaining paper files, this means they must audit, scan, and upload all requested documents to the auditor within two days.

### The Driver Qualification File Must Contain:

- DOT-Compliant Application
- Motor Vehicle Report
- Certificate of Road Test
- Medical Certificate (if non-CDL or in a non-participating state)
- Medical Examiner's National Registry Verification
- Skill Performance Evaluation Certificate Application (if applicable)

### The Safety Performance History Must Contain:

- Record of previous employers contacted
- Driver's written authorization to release information
