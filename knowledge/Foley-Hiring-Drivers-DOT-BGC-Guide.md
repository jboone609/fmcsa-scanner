## Hiring the Right Drivers with Thorough Background Checks

Before hiring a driver, run these required background checks:

| # | Check | Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Motor Vehicle Record (MVR) Reports** | Obtain MVRs from each state where the applicant held a license in the previous three years. Must be added to the DQF within 30 days. *Recordkeeping: Keep pre-employment MVR for duration of employment + 3 years.* |
| 2 | **Safety Performance Histories** | Investigate the driver's past three years of safety performance with all DOT-regulated employers. Must include: general identification/employment verification, crash details, and any minor accident information. |
| 3 | **FMCSA Clearinghouse Pre-Employment Queries** | Required before hiring a driver. Query the Clearinghouse database before the driver performs any safety-sensitive duties. *Recordkeeping: Keep records of queries for 3 years.* |
| 4 | **Commercial Driver's License Information System (CDLIS) Screens** | Includes the driver's current CDL number, issuing state, and up to three prior CDLs (including aliases/former names). Reveals if the driver held CDLs in other states warranting additional MVR checks. |
| 5 | **FMCSA Pre-Employment Screening Program (PSP)** | Contains a driver's most recent 5 years of crash data and 3 years of roadside inspection data. Reports employer, location, and whether the vehicle was placed out of service. |
| 6 | **Criminal Background Checks** | Uncovers felony or misdemeanor convictions, pending criminal cases, active warrants, infractions, and history of incarceration. |

## Pre-Employment DOT Drug Testing

**A new driver may not perform safety-sensitive duties — including operating a CMV — until they have been given a pre-employment drug test with a negative result.**

Prior to scheduling the test, inform the driver in writing of the testing requirements.

Key points:

- The DOT physical does not test for drugs — the urinalysis drug test is separate.
- Records of negative drug test results must be kept for **one year**.

---

## DOT Physical & Valid Medical Certificate

The DOT physical is a medical examination ensuring drivers are fit to meet the demands of the job and operate large vehicles without putting anyone at risk.

**Drivers must have a DOT physical if they:**

- Transport hazardous materials
- Operate a vehicle carrying more than 15 people
- Are paid to operate a vehicle carrying more than 8 people
- Operate a vehicle with a gross combined weight of more than 10,000 lbs

**Recordkeeping:**
- A medical certificate can be valid for up to **24 months**, depending on exam results.
- Drivers must undergo new DOT physicals before their certificates expire.
- All CLP and CDL drivers must keep the original or a copy of their medical card on their person while on duty.

## Building a Culture of Safety to Boost Driver Retention

According to research, companies with a strong onboarding process improve **new hire retention by 82%** and productivity by over 70%.

The DOT defines safety culture as *"shared values, actions, and behaviors that show a commitment to safety over competing goals and demands."*

**The perks of a healthy safety culture:**

- A better workplace for all employees — not just drivers
- Reduced risk of accidents
- Lower chance of lawsuits

If your employees see how much you prioritize their safety from day one, they're much more likely to carry those safety measures on the road with them.
