## Commercial Driver Inspection Standards

Before beginning a trip, the driver should know where all required documents are located — especially if kept in a digital format. Documents must be provided to an inspector quickly if selected for a Roadcheck inspection.

When an inspector interviews the driver, they must be able to provide:

- Current trip's starting point and final destination
- Load description
- Time traveled
- Most recent stop and fueling location(s)
- Any other jobs worked in the past week

The driver is also expected to assist with the activation of various vehicle systems during the inspection (lights, brakes, etc.).

## Driver Inspection Checklist

- [ ] **Seat belt** — The inspector will check the condition of the seat belt and ensure the driver is wearing it properly and it's fastened. *Note: Everyone in the CMV, including passengers and co-drivers, must be wearing a fastened seat belt.*

- [ ] **Driver condition** — The driver's overall condition will be observed for illness, fatigue, or other signs of impairment.

- [ ] **Unauthorized passengers** — The inspector will look for any unauthorized passengers.

- [ ] **Contraband** — The inspector will check for the illegal presence of alcohol, drugs, or other contraband.

- [ ] **Medical certificate & skill performance evaluation certificate** — Inspectors will verify validity and check for contact lenses, hearing aids, and any other physical limitations indicated on the driver's license.

- [ ] **Record of Duty Status (RODS)** — Drivers should have RODS and supporting documents ready. If using an ELD, the driver must be knowledgeable in its use and able to perform tasks asked by the inspector. The driver is required to transmit records upon request. The inspector will verify hours of service and check the accuracy of the record.

- [ ] **Exemptions** — If the driver is utilizing an exemption, inform the inspector. To avoid delays, print any exemptions and provide them to the inspector.

- [ ] **Driver's license / CDL** — The inspector will collect the driver's license or CDL and check the expiration date, class, endorsements, restrictions, and status.

- [ ] **FMCSA Drug and Alcohol Clearinghouse** *(U.S. Only)* — The inspector will query the FMCSA Clearinghouse to ensure the driver's license is not suspended or cancelled.
