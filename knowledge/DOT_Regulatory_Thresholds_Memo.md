# Memo: Understanding DOT Compliance Thresholds

---

## Why Understanding Compliance Thresholds Is Critical

- Different rules apply based on vehicle weight class
- Thresholds are based on the vehicle's **Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR)**
- Drives what Foley products apply
- Builds trust and credibility with the buyer

---

## CDL-Regulated Drivers – Full Compliance (Over 26,001 lbs GVWR)

Drivers operating vehicles over 26,001 lbs GVWR are required to hold a valid Commercial Driver's License (CDL). These drivers fall under full DOT compliance and require all three core programs.

- Driver Qualification File (DQF)
- DOT Drug & Alcohol Testing
- DOT Clearinghouse Queries

---

## DOT-Regulated (Non-CDL) Drivers (10,001 – 26,000 lbs GVWR)

This tier includes vehicles above 10,001 lbs but below 26,001 lbs GVWR. These do not require a CDL but still fall under DOT regulations.

- DQF (Med card, license, Safety Performance History)
- DOT Drug & Alcohol Testing — **not required**
- DOT Clearinghouse — **not applicable**

---

## Non-Regulated Drivers (Under 10,001 lbs GVWR)

Drivers operating light-duty vehicles under 10,001 lbs GVWR are not subject to DOT regulations but may still be monitored internally by employers.

- No DQF required
- No DOT Drug & Alcohol Testing
- No DOT Clearinghouse
- (Optional) MVR monitoring for internal risk management

---

## Where Foley Fits Across Thresholds
