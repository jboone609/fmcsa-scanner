## The Challenge

With tonnage on the rise, the demand for truck drivers has never been higher. Yet employers can't seem to staff up enough to keep all their trucks moving. With a turnover rate that has reached as high as **98 percent**, keeping drivers for longer than a few months is a challenge in itself.

The cost of hiring a new driver is over **$25,000**—an expense that adds up fast.

---

## The Root(s) of the Problem

Two primary factors make it challenging to hire and retain drivers:

### Driver Shortage
- The average truck driver is **47 years old**—the workforce is aging out
- An estimated shortage of more than **80,000 drivers** exists today
- That number could reach **170,000 by the end of 2030**

### Retention Issue
- Turnover stands at **98 percent**—over half of drivers leave within the first six months
- Experienced drivers know they have options and are getting more selective about who they work with

## Part 1: Attracting Drivers

### 1. Start Your Search in the Right Places

Job seekers are looking for work online—not in newspapers. **75% of truckers** check Facebook every day, and many use it to look for jobs. Post on social media and online job boards with a short, accurate job description and a link to an online application.

Don't forget job boards specifically designed for truck drivers, which are becoming increasingly popular.

### 2. Make It Mobile Friendly

Truck drivers are always on the go and are likely searching for jobs on a smartphone or tablet. Utilizing a mobile-friendly application has been shown to **increase response rate by 20%**.

Especially for the millennial generation, drivers are looking for more than a paycheck:

## Part 2: Evaluating the Best Talent

### 1. Evaluate Applications Quickly

The more time that passes between when a candidate applies and when you contact them, the less likely they'll still be available. Use an Applicant Tracking System to be alerted when new applications arrive so you can respond fast.

### 2. Do a Phone Screen First

A 10–15 minute call saves you the trouble of wasting time on a bad interview. It also helps break the ice before bringing them in. You'll be surprised at how much you can learn about a person in a short phone call.

### 3. Invite Them to an Interview

A face-to-face interview allows a more in-depth conversation about their background and the position. If the driver will work as part of a team, consider having them meet the people they'll work with daily.

### 4. Run Background Checks

A thorough background check isn't just important for DOT compliance—it verifies details from the application and interview process while helping protect the safety of your workforce. A pre-employment drug screen is also a DOT requirement for many drivers.

**Background screenings for regulated drivers should include:**
- Criminal background check
- Motor Vehicle Report in each state they've held a CDL
- DOT Clearinghouse query
- Commercial Driver's License Information System (CDLIS) search
- Pre-Employment Screening Program (PSP) screen
- Previous employer verification
- DOT pre-employment drug test
- Occupational Health physical

## Part 3: Retaining Employees Longer

### 1. Make the Most of Onboarding

Drivers who had a positive onboarding experience were **22% more likely** to stay during the first 6 months. Retention begins during the hiring process—not once they're behind the wheel.

**Tips:**
- Make it mobile friendly — applicants want to apply and complete background screening from their phone
- Be responsive — answer the phone or return calls quickly; good candidates will look elsewhere if they don't hear from you
- Get personal — build a connection by talking about family, hobbies, and other personal topics during the interview process

### 2. Make It a Positive Work Environment

Even with thorough screening, you may end up with a less-than-desirable driver. To identify problems early, have a **Motor Vehicle Report (MVR) and criminal monitoring program** in place. These programs monitor both records and alert you when changes are detected—so you know immediately if a driver is involved in an accident or has a suspended license.
