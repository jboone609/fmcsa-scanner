# Driver Qualification File Compliance Guide
*For Mixed Commercial Fleets (CDL & non-CDL) — 2025*

---

## Who Should Use This Guide

This guide is built for **mixed fleets** that operate a blend of CDL and non-CDL commercial motor vehicles (CMVs) across industries such as:

- Concrete/ready-mix
- Landscaping & tree care
- Construction trades
- Utilities/telecom
- Waste & recycling
- Fuel/oil (hazmat)
- Delivery/courier

It's written for HR, risk/safety/compliance managers, and operation/finance leaders who build, maintain, and audit driver files.

### Why It Matters

Driver qualification files (DQFs) aren't "paperwork for paperwork's sake." They protect your authority to operate, your insurance standing, and your brand. If you put a driver on the road without the proper license or file, you can face steep penalties and business risk.

> **One of the most expensive DOT violations is allowing a driver to operate without the correct license or class — a maximum fine of $34,712 for that single error.**

---

## Regulatory Snapshot (49 CFR Part 391)

Part 391 sets qualification standards for CMV drivers and requires specific hiring, screening, and record-keeping steps (Driver Qualification File + Safety Performance History). It applies to any carrier operating a vehicle in interstate commerce that:

- Weighs at least 10,001 pounds
- Carries more than 9 passengers
- Transports materials deemed hazardous under the Hazardous Materials Transportation Act

### Does My Driver Need a CDL?

A CDL is typically required when the vehicle:

- Has GVW/GVWR ≥ 26,001 lbs, **or**
- Is any size and requires hazmat placarding

**What you risk if you don't comply:** Regulatory fines, out-of-service headaches, higher insurance premiums, and heightened liability if an unqualified or incorrectly licensed driver is involved in a crash.

## Driver Categories & What Each Must Have

### CDL Class A/B Drivers
*(e.g., mixers, dumps, tractor-trailers, heavy service + trailer)*

**Minimum qualifications (Part 391):**
- 21+ years old; valid CMV license; physically qualified (DOT medical)
- Road test certificate (or equivalent)
- Ability to read/speak English for public/official interactions and to complete records
- Solid cargo securement understanding
- List of violations (past 12 months) or driver's certification
- Must not have disqualifying histories (on-duty D&A offenses, hit-and-run in CMV, felony involving a CMV)

**Files required:** DQF + SPH — both must be **100% complete within 30 days** of employment.

**Endorsements:** Tank, air brake, hazmat as applicable.

---

### CDL Class C
*(Passenger/Hazmat in smaller vehicles)*

Same Part 391 qualifications, DQF + SPH, and 30-day completion requirement.

**Common endorsements:** Passenger, Hazmat.

---

### Non-CDL CMV Drivers
*(10,001–26,000 GVWR or any size hazmat)*

Part 391 still applies: DQF + SPH, medical qualification, road test, annual MVR, and ongoing file maintenance.

**Examples:** Landscaping dump bodies, chipper trucks, service bodies towing equipment, small box trucks.

---

### Light-Duty, Non-Regulated Company Drivers
*(<10,001 lbs; non-hazmat; intrastate-only)*

Part 391 may not apply. Use a best-practice **"Company Driver File"**: short application, MVR consent and cadence, company road/ride-along notes, policy acknowledgments (seatbelts, handheld devices), and incident tracking.

## Hiring & Onboarding Workflow

### DOT-Compliant Employment Application (Day 0)

The application must include:

1. Your business name and address
2. Applicant's name, address, date of birth, SSN, and prior addresses (past 3 years)
3. Issuing state, number, and expiration date of each valid CMV license or permit
4. Detailed account of applicant's motor vehicle operating experience
5. Detailed list of all accidents in the past three years (date, nature, fatalities/injuries)
6. List of all motor vehicle violations in the past three years (excluding parking)
7. Statement detailing any denial, revocation, or suspension of license
8. List of all employers over the last three years (dates, reason for leaving, FMCSR subject)
9. Names and addresses of all CMV employers in the 7 years prior to the above 3-year period
10. Certification that the application was completed by the driver and is accurate

**Applicant rights:** Provide Summary of Rights and a dispute/rebuttal mechanism. Driver may request any time up to 30 days after hire/denial; employer must respond within 5 business days.

> **Risk if skipped:** Incomplete applications lead to missed red flags, failed audits, and FCRA issues.

---

### Verify Basic Requirements

All CMV drivers must:

- Be at least 21 years old (for interstate commerce)
- Have successfully completed a road test certificate (or equivalent)
- Have the ability to read and speak English

**Red-flag disqualifiers to watch for:**
- Suspended, revoked, or denied CMV license
- Drug and alcohol-related offenses while on duty
- History of leaving the scene of an accident while driving a CMV
- Prior felony involving the use of a CMV

> **Risk if skipped:** Ineligible drivers can invalidate coverage, trigger immediate enforcement action, and increase negligence claims after a crash.

---

### Conduct the DOT-Required Background Check Process

Required actions:

- **Motor Vehicle Report (MVR):** Request from each state where the driver held a license or permit in the last three years.
- **Safety Performance History:** Contact all DOT-regulated employers from the past three years to verify employment dates, accident history, and drug/alcohol program completion.
- **FMCSA Clearinghouse Query:** Run a pre-employment full query before the driver performs any safety-sensitive functions. The driver must provide electronic consent within the Clearinghouse.

**Best Practice Adds:**
- **CDLIS** to verify all active + up to three prior licenses
- **FMCSA PSP** (5 years crash, 3 years inspections)
- **FCRA-compliant criminal background screening**

> **Are you FCRA compliant?** From protecting against discrimination to providing applicants with a disclosure and Summary of Rights, there are steps you must take every time you run a background check.

## Creating the Files

At this point in the process, information must be organized into two folders to comply with DOT regulations.

### The Driver Qualification File (DQF) Must Include:

1. Employment application
2. Copy of CDL
3. Road certificate or CDL equivalent
4. Medical exam report
5. Medical Examiner's national registry verification
6. Skill performance eval (if needed)

### The Safety Performance History (SPH) File Must Include:

1. Driver's release authorization
2. Record of prior employers contacted (3 years)
3. Employee responses received
4. Drug & alcohol testing history (if applicable)
5. Proof of good-faith effort / no records available

> **Hiring Tip:** Both files must be created and 100% complete within **30 days** of a new driver's employment.

### Retention & Security

- Files must be kept for the duration of the driver's employment and **three years thereafter**.
- The Safety Performance History must be kept in a **secure location with limited access**.
- The DQF must be maintained on an ongoing basis — monitor document expiration dates and run an **annual MVR** for the driver.

---

## Ongoing Compliance & File Maintenance

Best practices for maintaining compliant files:

- Monitor expirations (CDL, med card, endorsements)
- Perform annual MVR + annual review; refresh violation lists
- Conduct **quarterly internal audits**: spot check DQF/SPH completeness, timeliness, and access controls
- Maintain an audit-ready binder for DOT
- Map reminders to your TMS/HRIS and maintain a roster risk list (30/60/90-day expiries)

> **Remember:** If it's not documented, it didn't happen — document road tests, refresher training, and any corrective actions.

## Industry-Specific Call-Outs

| Industry | Risk Hot Spots | Defense |
|---|---|---|
| **Concrete/Ready Mix** | PTO/chute lockout, backing at jobsites, surge with tank variants | Document jobsite-specific road tests, tank endorsements, and cargo securement procedures |
| **Landscaping & Tree Care** | Trailer brakes, equipment securement, chipper operations | Tow combo qualification notes in DQF; chipper/saw certificates; seasonal requalification |
| **Construction Trades** | Oversize/overweight permits, rigging/crane interfaces, work-zone control | File copies of permit procedures and rigging/flagging training |
| **Utilities/Telecom** | Aerial device use, energized-line proximity, storm callouts (HOS) | Aerial device training records and work-zone certifications |
| **Waste & Recycling** | PTO/hydraulic pinch points, route density (backing/side loads), spills | Route-specific road tests; spill response acknowledgments |
| **Delivery/Courier** | High-frequency stops, lift-gate injuries, cargo claims | Lift-gate training, parcel securement SOPs, incident response documentation |
| **Fuel/Oil & Other Hazmat** | Hazmat paperwork gaps, surge/baffle handling, spill events | Tank/hazmat endorsements and hazmat training records |

## Seasonal & Rehire Drivers

Seasonal gaps create expired credentials, lapsed training, and testing pool breaks. Use this quick-check before dispatch:

**License & Medical**
- [ ] CDL/license class & endorsements match assigned vehicle/load
- [ ] Medical card current and on file
- [ ] Annual MVR on file (or pull a fresh MVR if any doubt)

**Background & Testing — SPH/DQF**
- [ ] Up to date (update employer list, accident/violation declarations)
- [ ] Clearinghouse full query completed before safety-sensitive work (CDL only)
- [ ] Pre-employment drug test completed
- [ ] Re-enrolled in random testing pool; drug/alcohol policy acknowledgment reissued

**Road Test & Equipment Changes**
- [ ] Road test completed if: new vehicle class, trailer/towing added, or major equipment change
- [ ] Cargo securement refresher completed

**HOS/ELD Setup**
- [ ] Short-haul vs. ELD status confirmed for assignment
- [ ] If ELD required: device/app access and driver login reviewed

**Policies & Gear**
- [ ] Handbook/policy acknowledgments re-issued
- [ ] PPE issued/checked (industry-specific)
- [ ] Certifications current (chainsaw, rigging/flagging, confined space, etc.)

**Paperwork & Sign-offs**
- [ ] Seasonal Rehire Form completed
- [ ] Supervisor/dispatcher approval recorded in driver file
- [ ] 30/60/90-day alerts set for pending items

## Recap & Best Practices

**Your mixed fleet runs on proof — proof every driver is qualified, every file is current, and every trip is compliant.**

The core playbook:
1. Know when Part 391 applies
2. Decide early if a CDL (and which endorsements) is required
3. Hire and screen with discipline
4. Complete the DQF/SPH within 30 days
5. Keep files current with annual MVRs, medical card tracking, and tight SPH security
6. Layer in industry specifics and seasonal/rehire checks

**What's at stake if you don't?** Avoidable fines, out-of-service disruptions, insurance pressure, and tougher litigation if a crash occurs and your files can't prove due diligence.

> **Bottom Line:** Compliance isn't "paperwork"; it's risk control. Document what you do, verify before dispatch, and never put a driver on the road whose qualifications you can't prove on paper.
