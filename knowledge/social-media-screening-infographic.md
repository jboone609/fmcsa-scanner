# Social Media Screening: Why All the Fuss?

## Key Fact

U.S. social media users spend an average of **2 hours and 24 minutes per day** on social media.

> "Social media is no longer cutting-edge; it is mainstream. For HR to overlook it today would be like ignoring e-mail 20 years ago."
> — From *"Legal Trends: Social Media Use in Hiring: Assessing the Risks,"* SHRM

---

## True or False: It's OK for HR Managers to Conduct Social Media Screenings on Their Own.

**FALSE.**

There are significant legal implications if you conduct social media screening in-house. For example, if hiring managers handle screening internally, what happens when they come across protected class information? They can't "un-see" this information—which could expose the company to costly discrimination litigation.

---

## The Numbers

- **70%** of employers use social media to screen candidates (CareerBuilder)
- **54%** of employers chose not to hire a candidate because of what they found on social media (CareerBuilder)
