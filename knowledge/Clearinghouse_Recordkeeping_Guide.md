## Overview

The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) Clearinghouse makes it easier for DOT-regulated employers to see whether their new or current drivers have active drug and alcohol violations that prevent them from legally operating a commercial motor vehicle (CMV).

No longer do employers have to rely on a driver's stated employment history to ensure they don't have any drug or alcohol violations. A simple search instantly alerts employers to past violations and ensures that any driver put behind the wheel has completed the required return-to-duty process.

> Since the regulation went into effect, over **129,000 drug and alcohol violations** have been reported to the Clearinghouse — and the vast majority of drivers who have received a violation are still in prohibited status.

---

## Types of Clearinghouse Queries

Employers are required to check the Clearinghouse at the following times:
- **Once during the pre-employment process**
- **At least once annually** for the duration of each CDL driver's employment

The **pre-employment query MUST be a full query**. The annual query can be either a full or limited query.

---

### Limited Queries

- Only show that a record **exists** for a driver — not the details of the violation(s)
- Employers can use a **blanket consent form** to run these queries for the duration of employment
- If a limited query shows a record exists, an employer must run a **full query within 24 hours**
- If the driver doesn't consent, they must be pulled from safety-sensitive duties

---

### Full Queries

- Shows **all details** of the driver's record and violations
- Required as part of background checks before hiring a new CDL driver
- Employers must get **individual driver consent each time** via the Clearinghouse portal

---

## Consequences of Query Non-Compliance

Because the Clearinghouse is subject to the Fair Credit Reporting Act, there are consequences for running queries without consent.

> **In early 2021, the Department of Transportation set a fine of $5,833 for each violation** of any provisions in the Clearinghouse. This applies to all drivers, carriers, and medical review officers.

**Bottom line:** Follow the consent rules for all potential and current drivers.

---

## Query Best Practices

Running a limited query on drivers at **regular intervals throughout the year** is recommended. By running regular queries, you'll:
- Drastically reduce the chances of being surprised by a driver being pulled from duty during an inspection
- Better ensure that freight makes it to its destination safely and without delay

---

## Clearinghouse Recordkeeping Requirements

Companies must maintain a record of all DOT Clearinghouse checks, as well as a signed consent form for each driver.

All records must be stored in a **secure, easily accessible location** so they're readily available upon request by a DOT auditor.

### Document Retention Schedule

| Document | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Signed Limited Consent Forms | 3 Years |
| Pre-Employment Queries | 3 Years |
| Annual Queries | 3 Years |

### Why Digital Records Matter

- Paper files are extremely inefficient and prone to being misplaced or destroyed
- It's easy to overlook missing documents or expiration dates
- For offsite audits, all files must be scanned and sent electronically within 48 hours
- Any of these situations can result in DOT violations and fines

## 2023 DOT Clearinghouse Updates

### As of January 6, 2023:
- Companies **no longer need to reach out to prior employers** to confirm three years' worth of Safety Performance History data
- Companies **no longer need to keep external records** of Clearinghouse queries

### As of March 8, 2023:
- The Clearinghouse will **notify employers via email** if there is a change to a driver's Clearinghouse record for up to **12 months** following a pre-employment or annual query
- Triggering events include: new drug or alcohol violations, removed violations, and updated return-to-duty status
