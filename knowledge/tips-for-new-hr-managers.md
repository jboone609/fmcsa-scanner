# Hot Tips for New HR Managers
## 5 Questions to Ask About Your Employee Screening Process

When you start a new HR role, most pros review tools and systems in the first 90 days to find ways to streamline processes and rein in costs. One area that's sometimes overlooked: **employee screening**.

Here are five questions to ask during your audit of the employee screening process.

---

## 1. What's Your Average Time-To-Hire?

Are you using innovation and automation—like text messaging—to kick off the screening process so you can get people screened and working faster? Slow time-to-hire can mean losing top candidates to competitors.

---

## 2. Does Your Screening Process Include Contemporary Screens?

How long ago was the program put in place? Is there an opportunity to modernize it by adding social media screens to the mix? Or is there a way to streamline, thanks to technology?

---

## 3. Is Your Background Check Process Compliant?

Does the current screening process comply with federal, state, and local laws and regulations? Key questions to ask:
- Is there a clear and compliant adverse action process in place?
- How efficiently are disputes handled?
- How transparent is the dispute process?

---

## 4. What's the Rescreening Protocol?

If the company has only been conducting annual rescreens, does it make sense to consider **ongoing monitoring** instead? Rescreens and monitoring are not the same thing—monitoring provides continuous, real-time visibility into changes to an employee's record.

---

## 5. Is Your Screening Vendor PBSA Accredited?

The **Professional Background Screening Association (PBSA)** is the gold standard in the industry, and accreditation is rigorous. Always verify that your screening provider is PBSA accredited to ensure quality and compliance.
