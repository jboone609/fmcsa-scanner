## Introduction

Your drivers are one of your most valuable assets. They help to keep your business not only running, but successful and thriving.

Finding good, reliable, hard-working people can be challenging — especially in the trucking industry, where the driver shortage, workforce aging, and significant expected industry growth compound the difficulty.

When you find someone you're excited to bring on board, it's important to get the process right: both to start the relationship positively and to hire compliantly.

As a business operating under the DOT's Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA), you **must** comply with **49 CFR Part 391 – Qualifications of Drivers and Longer Combination Vehicle (LCV) Driver Instructors.** Best practices — including criminal background screens — are also strongly recommended.

---

## What is Regulation Part 391?

Regulation Part 391 (40+ pages) helps ensure that all federally-regulated motor carriers are qualified to be on the nation's roadways — in terms of physical health, professional experience, and motor vehicle record.

These regulations apply to any carrier operating a vehicle involved in interstate commerce that:

- Weighs at least 10,001 pounds
- Carries more than 9 passengers
- Transports materials deemed hazardous under the Hazardous Materials Transportation Act

Part 391 primarily covers:

- Qualification and Disqualification of Drivers
- Background and Character
- Tests
- Physical Qualifications and Examinations
- Files and Records

**Note for owner-operators:** You must serve as both boss and employee — maintaining records and performing follow-up screenings on yourself as a driver. However, a qualified person other than yourself must administer your road test.

## Step 1: Driver Application

To comply with DOT regulations, the application must be signed and dated by the applicant and include:

1. Your business name and address
2. Applicant's name, address, date of birth, Social Security Number, and any other addresses from the past three years
3. Issuing state, number, and expiration date of each valid CMV license or permit held
4. Detailed account of the applicant's motor vehicle operating experience
5. Detailed list of all accidents in the past three years (date, nature of crash, fatalities or injuries)
6. All motor vehicle violations in the past three years (excluding parking)
7. Statement about any denial, revocation, or suspension of license/permit
8. List of all employers over the last three years (dates, reason for leaving, whether subject to FMCSRs)
9. Names and addresses of all CMV employers from the seven years prior to the three-year period above
10. Certification that the application is complete and accurate

### Applicant Rights

Drivers must be given a **Summary of Rights**, informing them of the right to review information provided by previous employers and to dispute any findings. Drivers can submit a written request immediately upon applying or up to 30 days after being employed or denied. Prospective employers must provide information within **5 business days** of the request.

## Step 2: Driver Qualification

### Basic Requirements (per FMCSA)

All commercial motor vehicle drivers must:

- Be at least 21 years old
- Have the ability to read and speak English for official communications and recordkeeping
- Have successfully completed and received a road test certificate (or equivalent)
- Be physically qualified to drive a CMV and have a valid medical certificate
- Have a valid CMV license
- Have provided the carrier with a list of violations for the past 12 months or a recent Driver's Certification
- Have a solid understanding of cargo securement

### Disqualifying Factors

None of the following can be true of any candidate:

- A suspended, revoked, or denied CMV license
- Drug and alcohol-related offenses that occurred while on duty
- A history of leaving the scene of an accident while driving a CMV
- A prior felony involving the use of a CMV

## Step 3: Requirements Verification

### DOT-Required Background Check Process

The applicant must provide **written consent** before you can request information from prior employers. If they refuse, they cannot be legally allowed to operate a motor vehicle for your company.

Required background check steps:

1. **Motor Vehicle Report (MVR):** Request an MVR in each state where the driver has held a license or permit in the last three years.
2. **Prior employer contact:** Contact all DOT-regulated employers from the past three years to verify dates of employment, accidents, drug/alcohol violations, and completion of rehabilitation programs.

All responses must be documented, including each employer's name, address, and the date of contact. If an employer cannot be reached, document all attempts. The absence of MVR or employment history must also be documented.

## Step 4: File Creation

Once background checks are complete, organize information into two required folders (both must be 100% complete within **30 days** of a new driver's employment):

### Driver Qualification File (DQF) must include:

1. Application for Employment
2. Commercial Driver License
3. Motor Vehicle Report (MVR)
4. Certificate of Road Test
5. Medical Examination Report
6. Medical Examiner's National Registry Verification
7. Skill Performance Evaluation Certificate Application

### Safety Performance History (SPH) file must include:

1. Record of Previous Employers Contacted
2. Driver's Written Authorization to Release Information

Both files must be kept for the duration of the driver's employment and **three years thereafter**. The Safety Performance History must be kept in a **secure location with limited access**.

The Driver Qualification File must be maintained on an ongoing basis, including monitoring document expiration dates and running an annual MVR.

## Step 5: Best Practices

While not always required, these additional checks are strongly recommended:

### Commercial Driver's License Information System (CDLIS)

Running a CDLIS search verifies that CDL history on the application is thorough and accurate. This nationwide database provides details on all active licenses plus three prior licenses, giving a more comprehensive view of motor vehicle history.

### FMCSA Pre-Employment Screening Program (PSP)

A PSP provides **5 years' worth of crash data** and **3 years of inspection history** — significantly more detail than standard prior employment verifications.

### Criminal Background Check

Although not required by the DOT, criminal background checks are an important best practice. They allow you to search for felony, misdemeanor, or other offenses at the county, state, and federal levels.

## Step 6: Moving Forward

Once background checks and driver files are complete, ongoing steps are required to keep files compliant — including gathering reports and monitoring expiration dates. This requires high organizational discipline, especially for larger fleets.
