## Trucking Violation Fines Have Gone Up — Again.

On December 30, 2024, the FMCSA passed a final rule establishing higher violation fines for 2025. This increase is only adding to the ever-rising costs in the trucking industry, such as insurance premiums, CDL speeding ticket fines, and accidents.

**Staying on top of your DOT compliance is more important than ever.**

We've provided some of the most prevalent commercial vehicle, recordkeeping, and CDL violations; the 2024 fines; and the new fines for 2025 to help you be proactive, not reactive, to this federal update.

---

## 2025 FMCSA Fine Increases

*Source: www.federalregister.gov/documents/2023/12/28/2023-28066/revisions-to-civil-penalty-amounts-2024*

| Violation | 2024 Fine | 2025 Fine |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| OOS order (requiring or permitting operation of CMV by driver) | $23,048 | $23,647 |
| OOS order (operation by driver of CMV/intermodal equipment placed OOS) | $2,304 | $2,364 |
| OOS order (failure to return certification of correction) | $1,152 | $1,182 |
| OOS order (failure to cease operations as ordered) | $33,252 | $34,116 |
| OOS order (op. during suspension or revocation, penalties unpaid) | $18,759 | $19,246 |
| Employer authorizing employee violations of OOS order (max penalty) | $38,612 | $39,615 |
| Recordkeeping: Maximum penalty per day | $1,544 | $1,584 |
| Recordkeeping: Maximum total penalty | $15,445 | $15,846 |
| Knowing falsification of records | $15,445 | $15,846 |
| Commercial driver's license (CDL) violations | $6,974 | $7,155 |
