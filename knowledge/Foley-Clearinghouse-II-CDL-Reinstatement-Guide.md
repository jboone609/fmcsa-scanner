# Recovering from the FMCSA Clearinghouse-II: How to Reinstate CDLs After State-Issued License Downgrades

*By Foley | foleyservices.com | (860) 791-4564*

## The New FMCSA Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse-II Regulation

**As of November 18, 2024...**

State Driver Licensing Agencies (SDLAs) must remove the commercial driving privileges from the driver's license of any individual with a "prohibited" FMCSA Clearinghouse status until the driver completes the return-to-duty (RTD) process.

**This means any drivers with unresolved FMCSA drug and/or alcohol Clearinghouse violations will lose or be denied the use of their CDLs or CLPs.**

Some SDLAs may have begun the process prior to the November 18 deadline if they had the legislative authority to query the FMCSA Clearinghouse and act upon their findings.

If a driver is "prohibited" in the FMCSA Clearinghouse or faces a license downgrade, they must complete the DOT return-to-duty process involving evaluation, treatment, and testing, managed by a professional, to regain their commercial driving privileges.

**Now is the time to ensure your drivers are not impacted by this new regulation, and if they are, they must begin the return-to-duty process immediately.**

---

## State Department Licensing Agencies CDL Downgrade Letters

Most, if not all, State Department Licensing Agencies (SDLAs) have started sending commercial driver's license (CDL) downgrade letters via US mail to those drivers with "prohibited" Clearinghouse statuses under the new Clearinghouse-II regulation.

It's important to note that all CDL/CLP transactions, including upgrades, renewals, and duplicates will be denied until the driver becomes compliant. This also includes reciprocities.

**Each state has its own specific requirements for reinstating CDLs, but all 50 US states require commercial drivers in "prohibited" status to complete the DOT return-to-duty process before applying for a new CDL.**

## Enrolling "Prohibited" Drivers in the DOT Return-to-Duty Process

If you run a query on any of your drivers and reveal they have a record in the Clearinghouse, they must be immediately removed from their safety-sensitive duties, including driving, and complete the return-to-duty (RTD) process before they can return to these responsibilities.

### What Is the Return-to-Duty Process?

The DOT requires the following steps to be taken after a CDL driver commits a drug or alcohol violation:

1. **Removal from safety-sensitive functions immediately** — The driver must stop driving as soon as it's safe to do so.
2. **Initial evaluation with a DOT-qualified Substance Abuse Professional (SAP)** — During this face-to-face interview, the SAP will evaluate the employee to understand their situation and usage history, and determine what type of activities or treatment the employee must undergo.
3. **Education and treatment program** — The SAP will prescribe a treatment program for the employee based on their initial interview.
4. **SAP follow-up evaluation** — After the employee has completed the SAP treatment program, another evaluation must take place with the same SAP. At that time, they will determine whether the employee may take a RTD test and resume safety-sensitive functions.
5. **Return-to-duty drug test** — Once the SAP confirms that the employee has completed all requirements, they'll recommend to the DER that the employee take a RTD test. The collection of the specimen must be directly observed by an individual of the same sex.
6. **Follow-up test** — The SAP will provide the Designated Employer Representative (DER) with a schedule of follow-up tests.
7. **Returning to duty** — Once a negative DOT test result is received, the employee may resume their safety-sensitive functions, such as driving.
8. **FMCSA Clearinghouse reporting** — Employers must report negative RTD test results to the Clearinghouse by the close of the third business day following the date on which the results were received.

## The DOT Return-to-Duty Recordkeeping Requirements

In addition to complying with the federal RTD regulations, you must also ensure you're staying on track with all records associated with the return to duty process. **You must have a written DOT-compliant drug and alcohol testing program in place that includes RTD/follow-up testing procedures for the duration of time it was enforced, plus five years after it has been replaced or revised.**

### Keep the Following Records for Five Years:

- Positive alcohol test results indicating an alcohol concentration of 0.02 or greater
- Verified positive drug test results
- Refusals to take required alcohol and/or drug tests (including substituted or adulterated drug test results)
- SAP reports
- All follow-up tests and schedules for follow-up tests

Remember, all DOT drug and alcohol testing records must be kept in a secure location with controlled access. Password-protect your electronic records. Ensure all records are legible and signatures can be authenticated in the event of an audit.

## Reinstating CDLs After Completing the DOT Return-to-Duty Process

Once a driver completes the DOT return-to-duty process, and their Clearinghouse status is updated to "not prohibited," their State Driver Licensing Agency (SDLA) will allow them to reinstate their commercial driving privileges as long as they have not exceeded the maximum number of drug or alcohol violations according to state laws.

> **Please note:** The information below is provided to assist impacted CDL holders with the necessary state requirements, but it is not meant to be all-inclusive. Please check with your local SDLA office for the most accurate information.

### State CDL Reinstatement Process

| State | CDL Reinstatement Process |
|-------|--------------------------|
| **AL** | A driver must wait until their CDL disqualification period has terminated before reinstating their CDL, then apply to have their CDL reinstated and pay the $100 reinstatement fee. *(Source: www.alabamatrucking.org)* |
| **AR** | If a driver's commercial license is downgraded or voluntarily surrendered for one (1) year or more, they must retake all phases of the CDL exam. *(Source: https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/)* |
| **AZ** | To reinstate their license, a driver must pay a $10 suspension fee and application fee. If the AZMDC does not have a digital photo of the driver on file, they must apply in person. Otherwise, they may pay these fees by mail or online. *(Source: www.azdot.gov/mdv)* |
| **CA** | A driver may renew their CDL 24 hours after their DACH status is "not prohibited." If they would like to upgrade to a CDL after a downgrade, then the knowledge and skills/drive tests will be required. Fees may apply. *(Source: www.dmv.ca.gov)* |
| **CO** | Drivers may be required to take both the regular and CDL written exams and drive skills tests after they reinstate their CDL or CLP. *(Source: www.dmv.colorado.gov)* |
| **CT** | Drivers may upgrade their driver's license to a CDL at a local DMV office after completing the DOT return-to-duty process. The fee to upgrade a CDL is $22. Applicable knowledge and skills/driving tests may need to be completed. *(Source: www.portal.ct.gov)* |
| **FL** | In most cases, the driver must wait out the disqualification period and pay the pre-determined disqualification reinstatement fee to restore their CDL. *(Source: www.flhsmv.org)* |
| **GA** | A driver upgrading an existing CDL must first obtain a Commercial Permit (CLP). They must take the required CDL Knowledge Test (written exam) and hold the permit for 14 days before taking the road test. Additional fees may also be required to obtain CDL privileges. *(Source: www.dds.georgia.gov)* |
| **IA** | After one year following a CDL downgrade, the driver must take the knowledge and driving tests. The driver must pay the reinstatement fee and license fee as well. *(Source: www.iowadot.gov/)* |
| **IL** | If a CDL holder downgrades their class of CDL, they will be required to conduct all applicable knowledge/written and skills/driving testing in a representative vehicle to regain the classification. Applicable fees must also be paid. *(Source: www.ilsos.gov)* |
| **IN** | If a driver's CDL has been expired, disqualified, canceled, revoked, voluntarily surrendered, relinquished, or invalidated for over three years, they must meet all application requirements as if they are applying for an initial CDL, including applicable knowledge exams and CDL skills tests. *(Source: www.in.gov)* |
| **KS** | A driver must first complete the reinstatement application, submit proof of successful completion of a drug/alcohol rehabilitation program, and pay any applicable reinstatement fees. *(Source: www.ksrevenue.gov)* |
| **KY** | Drivers generally need to pay a reinstatement fee and potentially retake the written and vision tests if their CDL privileges have been suspended for over a year. *(Source: www.drive.ky.gov)* |
| **LA** | If the license is downgraded for one year, the driver must pass the knowledge exam required for the license class and endorsement(s) to upgrade. If two years, the driver is also required to pass a skills test in a vehicle of the same class for which the license is being upgraded. *(Source: www.louisiana.gov)* |
| **MA** | A CDL driver must take both the CDL permit exam and the CDL road test in order to reinstate his/her suspended/revoked CDL learner's permit or license. Reinstatement fees range from $100.00 to $1200.00. *(Source: www.mass.gov)* |
| **MD** | After a disqualification period of less than one year, a driver may apply for a CDL reinstatement without taking the skills and knowledge tests relevant to their previous CDL. The CDL will be reissued when the driver pays the required fees. *(Source: www.mva.maryland.gov)* |
| **MI** | Drivers may need to complete applicable knowledge/written and skills/driving testing to reinstate their CDL privileges. The standard reinstatement fee is $125. *(Source: www.michigan.gov)* |
| **MN** | A driver applicant for a CDL whose license has been downgraded to a class D license for more than one year must pass applicable required knowledge tests and a road test administered by the commissioner. *(Source: www.revisor.mn.gov)* |
| **MO** | Prior CDL holders who remain in a prohibited status and apply for a noncommercial license at time of renewal will be required to complete all knowledge and skills testing to obtain a CDL. *(Source: www.dor.mo.gov)* |
| **MS** | Drivers can visit an MSDPS to reinstate their commercial driver's license and/or commercial learner permit. Reinstatement fees range from $100–$175. *(Source: www.driverservicebureau.dps.ms.gov)* |
| **NC** | Drivers can visit an NCDMV to reinstate their commercial driver's license and/or commercial learner permit. The NCDMV may require retesting and additional fees to restore their commercial driver's license privileges. *(Source: www.ncdot.gov)* |
| **NJ** | Drivers can pay the $100 restoration fee and restore their license online, in person, or by mail. *(Source: www.nj.gov)* |
| **NV** | Drivers must pass all applicable knowledge and skills tests, complete a CDL application, pay all required fees, and be issued and hold a new CLP for 14 days before taking any skills test. *(Source: www.dmv.nv.gov)* |
| **NY** | New non-CDL documents will be mailed to downgraded drivers. Drivers who have had their CLP or CDL downgraded or canceled must take any tests and pay any fees associated with the upgrade or receipt of a new document. *(Source: www.dmv.ny.gov)* |
| **OH** | Drivers will be required to serve the disqualification terms according to their convictions and pay determined reinstatement fees to restore their CDL privileges. *(Source: www.bmv.ohio.gov)* |
| **OK** | Drivers can reinstate commercial licenses online. Once the request is submitted, a Service Oklahoma Compliance Officer will reach out with more details to complete the reinstatement. *(Source: www.oklahoma.gov)* |
| **OR** | To determine their reinstatement requirements, the driver must log in to their DMV driving record account at DMV2U. A CDL upgrade fee of $160 may apply. *(Source: www.oregon.gov)* |
| **PA** | In addition to serving the time required to the disqualification, drivers will have to pay a restoration fee to PennDOT before their CDL will be returned. *(Source: www.pa.gov)* |
| **SC** | Drivers who revert to a non-commercial status will not be required to retake CDL tests once the disqualification has ended unless their CDL has been disqualified for a year or more. A $100 reinstatement fee must be paid to the SCDMV. *(Source: www.scdmvonline.com)* |
| **TN** | Reinstatement and requirement information is included in the letter(s) impacted drivers received from the Department of Safety and Homeland Security regarding CDL downgrades. *(Source: www.tn.gov)* |
| **TX** | After receiving a request for reinstatement, a driver's record will be reviewed for eligibility and a final determination will be sent via mail. If they are approved for reinstatement, they must apply as an original CDL applicant at their local driver's license office and pay applicable fees. *(Source: www.dps.texas.gov)* |
| **UT** | After the Utah Driver License Division has received Clearinghouse documentation, the driver must pay a reinstatement fee. *(Source: www.dld.utah.gov)* |
| **VA** | If a CDL has been downgraded for less than one year, the driver can resume operating commercial vehicles, provided all requirements imposed by the DMV or a court have been resolved. If the CDL has been downgraded or surrendered for over a year, the driver must complete the CDL knowledge and road skills tests. *(Source: www.dmv.virginia.gov)* |
| **WA** | If a driver has been without a CDL for less than two years, they can reinstate without any additional testing or training. Additional fees may apply. *(Source: www.dol.wa.gov)* |
| **WI** | Once the Wisconsin DMV receives updated information from the Clearinghouse database indicating the driver's status is "Not Prohibited", if no other outstanding issues exist, the commercial classes and endorsements will return to a valid status. *(Source: www.wisconsindot.gov)* |
