## Introduction

The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) uses seven **Behavior Analysis and Safety Improvement Categories (BASICs)** to determine a motor carrier's safety performance and compliance in comparison with other carriers. Five BASICs are available to the public online in the **Safety Measurement System (SMS)**.

### The Seven BASICs:
1. Vehicle Maintenance
2. Unsafe Driving
3. Crash Indicator
4. Hazardous Materials (HM)
5. Driver Fitness
6. Controlled Substance/Alcohol
7. Hours-of-Service (HOS)

A carrier's performance under each of these BASICs contributes to their CSA scores, which can influence insurance premiums and business partnerships — for better or for worse.

### How Do Carriers Know Where They Stand?

The SMS determines an overall BASIC status for each motor carrier based on roadside inspection results reflected as a percentile rank. **Violations related to any of the BASICs can result in safety interventions, including warning letters and investigations.**

Once violations are committed, they will affect a motor carrier's **SMS status for 24 months**. Only time and/or inspections without violations in that specific BASIC will improve their percentile rank.

## BASIC #1 — Vehicle Maintenance

This BASIC addresses the requirements within 49 CFR Parts 392, 393, and 396, to properly maintain a commercial motor vehicle (CMV) and prevent shifting loads, spilled or dropped cargo, and overloading.

### Violations Included in the Vehicle Maintenance BASIC
A motor carrier may rank poorly if they allow a driver to operate an out-of-service vehicle or one with inoperative brakes, lights, and/or other mechanical defects, and do not complete necessary repairs. Improperly securing loads is also a cause of poor performance.

### Vehicle Maintenance Recordkeeping Requirements

During an investigation, a carrier may need to produce:
- Roadside inspection reports
- Vehicle maintenance files
- Annual vehicle inspection reports
- Driver Vehicle Inspection Reports (DVIRs)
- Equipment repair receipts
- Evidence of driver training on load securement
- Accident reports

Records per 49 CFR 396.3 must include:
- Identifying information (company number, make, serial number, year, tire size)
- A schedule of inspection and maintenance types and due dates
- Records of dates and nature of each inspection, repair, and maintenance

### Vehicle Maintenance Record Retention Requirements
- Maintain records for **30 consecutive days**
- Keep where the vehicle is stored for **one year**
- Retain for **6 months** after the vehicle is no longer owned or leased

Passenger carriers must inspect emergency doors, marking lights, and pushout windows **every 90 days** and keep all records.

### Quick Vehicle Maintenance Recordkeeping Checklist
- [ ] Roadside inspection reports
- [ ] Vehicle maintenance files
- [ ] Annual vehicle inspection reports
- [ ] Driver Vehicle Inspection Reports (DVIRs)
- [ ] Equipment repair receipts
- [ ] Evidence of driver training on load securement
- [ ] Accident reports
- [ ] Records of emergency doors, marking lights, and windows inspections (every 90 days — passenger carriers only)

## BASIC #2 — Unsafe Driving

This BASIC addresses FMCSA requirements 49 CFR Parts 392 & 397. It refers to the unsafe operation of CMVs in a dangerous or careless manner.

A motor carrier may rank poorly if any drivers are caught speeding, driving recklessly, changing lanes improperly, or driving distracted.

### Unsafe Driving Recordkeeping & Retention Requirements
- **MVRs** must be run on applicants for the previous three years in all states where the applicant held a license, and no less than once every 12 months for all employees.
- Employers must require drivers to submit a **list of any moving violations** for the previous 12 months annually.
- For drivers transporting Division 1.1, 1.2, or 1.3 explosives — a **written route plan** is required.

### Quick Unsafe Driving Recordkeeping Checklist
- [ ] MVRs — run on applicants for previous three years and annually for all employees
- [ ] List of moving violations — annually for all employees
- [ ] Written route plan — if driver is transporting explosives

## BASIC #3 — Crash Indicator

This BASIC is the only category that does not use violations of specific regulations. Having a history of frequent and severe accidents can cause carriers to rank poorly.

> **Note:** Information regarding a carrier's performance in the Crash Indicator BASIC is **not available to the public.** Only a motor carrier logged into its own safety profile or enforcement personnel may access it.

### Crash Indicator Recordkeeping & Retention Requirements

Per 49 CFR 390.5T, **motor carriers must maintain a register of all crashes that occurred within the past three years.** The register must contain, at minimum:
- Date of the crash
- City/town and state closest to the crash location
- Driver's name
- Number of injuries or fatalities
- Whether hazardous materials (other than fuel) were released

If a crash causes the release of hazardous materials, **carriers must complete a hazardous incident report** per 49 CFR 171.15 and 49 CFR 171.16.

### Quick Crash Indicator Recordkeeping Checklist
- [ ] Register of all crashes from the past three years
- [ ] Incident reports for all crashes involving hazardous materials

## BASIC #4 — Hazardous Materials (HM) Compliance

This BASIC addresses 49 CFR Part 397 and Parts 171–180 of the Hazardous Materials Regulations (HMRs). It ensures hazardous materials are safely transported on CMVs.

> **Note:** HM Compliance BASIC information is **not available to the public.** Carriers that do not handle hazardous materials receive a zero in this category.

A motor carrier may rank poorly if a driver fails to mark, label, placard, or secure a package containing HM in accordance with set regulations.

### HM Compliance Recordkeeping & Retention Requirements

**Incident Reports:** Complete for all crashes involving the release of HM.

**Shipping Papers:**
- Hazardous waste: retain copies for **3 years** after the material is accepted
- All other HM: retain copies for **2 years** after acceptance

**HM Training Records** must be kept for the duration of employment and **90 days** after employment ends. Each record must include:
- Employee's name
- Most recent training completion date
- Description, copy, or location of training materials
- Trainer's name and address
- Certification that the employee was trained and tested

### Quick HM Compliance Recordkeeping Checklist
- [ ] Incident reports for all crashes involving the release of HM
- [ ] Shipping papers (hazardous waste: 3 years; other HM: 2 years)
- [ ] Evidence of HM training
- [ ] Hazardous waste manifests
- [ ] Cargo tank manufacturer's certificates

## BASIC #5 — Driver Fitness

This BASIC addresses FMCSA requirements 49 CFR Parts 383 & 391. It refers to the unsafe operation of CMVs by drivers who are unfit due to a lack of training, experience, or medical qualifications.

### Driver Fitness Recordkeeping & Retention Requirements

Two categories of required files: **DQFs and Safety Performance History.**

Carriers must keep DQFs on all drivers who operate CMVs in interstate commerce that weigh 10,001+ lbs, transport hazardous materials, or carry 10+ passengers.

**DQF must include:**
- Employment application — duration of employment + 3 years
- MVR/Annual review of driving record — MVR for duration of employment + 3 years; annual review for 3 years
- Annual list of violations — 3 years
- Road test certificate — duration of employment + 3 years
- Medical examination certificate — updated at least every 24 months, maintained for 3 years
- Skill performance evaluation certificate (for drivers with missing/impaired limbs) — valid for 2 years
- Medical examiner's National Registry verification — duration of employment + 3 years

**Safety Performance History must include:**
- Record of previous employers contacted
- Written driver consent to release safety-sensitive information

### Driver Automatic Disqualification Criteria
- Suspended, revoked, or denied CMV license
- On-duty drug and/or alcohol-related offenses
- History of leaving an accident scene while driving a CMV
- Prior felony involving a CMV

### Quick Driver Fitness BASIC Recordkeeping Checklist
- [ ] Employment application — duration of employment + 3 years
- [ ] MVR/Annual review of driving record
- [ ] Annual list of violations — 3 years
- [ ] Road test certificate — duration of employment + 3 years
- [ ] Medical examination certificate — updated every 24 months, keep for 3 years
- [ ] Skill performance evaluation certificate — valid for 2 years
- [ ] Record of previous employers contacted
- [ ] Written driver consent to release safety-sensitive information
- [ ] Medical examiner's National Registry verification — duration of employment + 3 years

> **Note:** For the past five years, failing to create and maintain DQFs has accounted for **20% of critical and acute violations.**

## BASIC #6 — Controlled Substances/Alcohol

This BASIC refers to regulation 49 CFR Parts 382 and 392, which requires carriers to have specific programs in place to help prevent accidents and injuries resulting from alcohol misuse or use of controlled substances.

### Violations Included
- Failing a random drug test
- Alcohol test with BAC level of .02 or higher
- Operating under the influence of illegal drugs

### Controlled Substances/Alcohol Recordkeeping Requirements

Per 49 CFR part 382.401, employers must maintain records of:
- Drug and alcohol test results
- Testing process administration
- Return-to-duty process administration
- Employee training
- Supervisor training

All records must be kept in a **secure location with controlled access.** Electronic records must be password protected.

### Record Retention Schedule

**Keep for 5 years:**
- Previous pre-employment testing (with violations)
- Positive alcohol test results (≥ 0.02)
- Verified positive drug test results
- Refusals to take required tests
- SAP reports
- All follow-up tests and schedules
- Testing service provider contracts
- MIS/calendar year summaries
- Semi-annual lab summaries

**Keep for 3 years:**
- Previous pre-employment testing (no violations)
- DOT Clearinghouse limited consent forms
- DOT Clearinghouse pre-employment queries
- DOT Clearinghouse annual queries

**Keep for 2 years:**
- Driver roster for random testing pull
- Names selected for random tests
- Tracking drivers who were tested

**Keep for 1 year:**
- Negative and canceled drug and alcohol test results with a concentration of less than 0.02

**Duration-based retention:**
- DOT drug and alcohol policy — duration enforced + 5 years after replacement
- Signed receipt for policy and educational materials — duration of employment + 2 years
- Supervisor reasonable suspicion training — duration of employment + 2 years
- In-house specimen collector or breath alcohol technician training — duration of employment + 2 years

## BASIC #7 — Hours-of-Service (HOS) Compliance

The HOS Compliance BASIC is based on regulations at 49 CFR Part 392 and Part 395. It covers records of duty status (RODS) and the management of CMV driver fatigue.

If drivers operate their CMVs over their allotted hours of service and/or falsify their RODS, they are not complying with the BASIC and can receive FMCSA violations.

### HOS BASIC Recordkeeping & Retention Requirements

A motor carrier must retain drivers' **records of duty status (RODS)** and supporting documents (dispatch records, receipts, statements, bills of lading, weight station tickets, or phone bill statements) for **six months** after they are received.

A **backup copy of ELD records** must be maintained on a separate device for **six months** as well.

For short-haul HOS, employers must document:
- Driver's clock-in and clock-out times
- Total time worked during the current period
- Total time worked during the previous seven days

### Quick Hours-of-Service BASIC Recordkeeping Checklist

*Maintain the following records for 6 months:*
- [ ] Drivers' RODs and supporting documents
- [ ] Backup copy of ELD records
- [ ] Short-haul HOS records
