## 1. Roadcheck 2025 Focus Areas

From **May 13–15, 2025**, CVSA-certified law enforcement personnel will conduct thorough inspections of commercial vehicles and drivers at various locations, including weigh/inspection stations, temporary sites, and mobile patrols throughout North America.

**The 2025 CVSA International Roadcheck will focus on tires and false records of duty status.**

- **Hours of service** was the number one driver violation category cited during last year's Roadcheck inspections — 870 violations were cited, making up 32% of total driver violations.
- **Tires** was the number two mechanical violation category cited during the 2024 Roadcheck inspections. Inspectors found 2,821 tire violations, accounting for 20% of total mechanical violations.

Roadcheck 2025 serves as a reminder to companies with DOT-regulated drivers to establish and strictly enforce clear policies to prevent driver, vehicle, and FMCSA compliance violations. Owner-operators, Safety Managers, and Compliance Managers are encouraged to regularly query the FMCSA Clearinghouse to ensure their drivers are not in a prohibited status, which now revokes their CDL/CPL privileges.

### Top Tips to Prepare for Roadcheck 2025

- Run motor vehicle record (MVR) checks on drivers to avoid surprise CDL/CLP violations
- Remind drivers to perform pre- and post-trip inspections during every run
- Ensure your Electronic Logging Devices (ELDs) are compliant and operating properly
- Query CDL drivers in the FMCSA Clearinghouse to uncover violations and prohibited statuses

## 2. Run Driver MVR Checks Before Roadcheck 2025

As a DOT-regulated business, you're well-accustomed to the process of pulling annual MVRs for every driver you employ. You must do so to meet federal recordkeeping requirements.

**Running MVR checks on your drivers before Roadcheck 2025 can help avoid surprise traffic or safety violations being revealed during the inspection process.**

The challenge: What happens when one of your drivers gets a DUI or commits a traffic or safety violation soon after you pull their annual report? Or if they have their license suspended for another significant offense? Unless they voluntarily come clean with new information on infractions or violations, you'll be in the dark until the next annual review or audit.

For a growing number of companies, the solution is **MVR Monitoring**. These automated programs actively monitor the MVRs for CDL drivers on an ongoing basis to help businesses more easily identify problems, address concerns, and put corrective action plans in place before an out-of-service issue arises.

### How Does MVR Monitoring Work?

MVR Monitoring keeps tabs on driving records. When there is a change, employers are immediately alerted to any "hit" and receive the updated MVR for review.

These "hits" could be anything from accidents or DUIs to speeding tickets or other violations or convictions. They could also signify CDL suspensions or revocations, safety violations, or medical certificate downgrades. They include positive changes as well, such as when a driver renews or updates their CDL or medical certificate.

## 3. Ensure Your DOT Drug & Alcohol Testing Program Is Compliant

According to federal law, all CDL drivers considered "safety-sensitive" employees must participate in a DOT-compliant drug and alcohol testing program. This includes drivers who:

- Operate vehicles weighing at least 26,001 pounds
- Transport hazardous materials
- Transport vehicles with at least 16 passengers

To ensure full compliance, all drivers listed above must participate in both a comprehensive drug and alcohol testing program **and** the FMCSA Drug and Alcohol Clearinghouse.

### Comprehensive DOT Drug & Alcohol Testing Programs Must Include:

- Pre-employment testing
- Post-accident testing
- Random testing
- Reasonable suspicion testing
- Return-to-duty/follow-up testing

Violations of any of the above — such as positive DOT drug test results or test refusals — must then be reported to the FMCSA Clearinghouse.

> **Pro Tip:** Enrolling drivers in a DOT-compliant drug and alcohol testing program before Roadcheck 2025 can help prevent violations related to last year's alcohol and controlled substance possession focus area.

## 4. Query the FMCSA Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse

Employers are required to check the Clearinghouse once during the pre-employment process and at least once annually for the duration of each CDL driver's employment.

Running a limited query on each of your drivers before Roadcheck 2025 allows you to get ahead of any drug or alcohol violations reported to the Clearinghouse. If an inspector finds violations, the driver will be removed from their safety-sensitive duties until they have completed the return-to-duty process.

### Limited Clearinghouse Queries

Limited queries only show that a violation record exists for a driver — it doesn't show the details of the violation(s).

Employers can use a blanket consent form to run these queries. This consent form can remain in effect for the duration of a driver's employment, allowing them to run limited queries on drivers as often as they'd like.

If a limited query shows that a record exists for a driver, then an employer is required to run a full query within 24 hours. If the driver doesn't consent to the query, they will need to be pulled from safety-sensitive duties.

> **Reminder:** A new Clearinghouse regulation officially went into effect on November 18, 2024 that directly impacted drivers with a "prohibited" status in the Clearinghouse. Those with this status had their CDLs/CLPs revoked or denied.
