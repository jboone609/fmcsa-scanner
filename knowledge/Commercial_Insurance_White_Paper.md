# Commercial Insurance Mayhem: How to Stop the Bleeding
**How Transportation Companies & Insurance Agents Get Underwriters to Improve Their Risk Profile**

*A White Paper in Collaboration with Keystone & Foley — Spring 2024*

---

## The Hard Truth About This Hard Market

Whether you're a fleet owner, safety manager, or commercial insurance agent, we're in a hard insurance market. Premiums are skyrocketing due to social inflation, extreme weather events, and parts shortages. Insurance coverage is dwindling or becoming non-existent, with no sign of the marketplace softening any time soon.

Timothy Weaverling, a partner and commercial account executive with Reed, Wertz & Roadman (RWR) in Pennsylvania, reports seeing double-digit premium increases for his school bus transportation clients — as high as 20% on a single account, representing a $100,000 increase.

### What Can Carriers and Insurance Agents Do?

While you can't control Mother Nature or inflation, there is a specific action that can help you negotiate more reasonable insurance premiums — and possibly even lower them.

**The solution:** Use hard data to demonstrate to underwriters exactly how you're actively improving your risk profile. Underwriters ultimately care about one thing: How big of a risk are you? The bigger the risk, the bigger the premium.

---

## How We Got Here & What It Means for Underwriting

According to the Insurance Information Institute, social inflation has led to a **$30 billion surge in commercial auto claim costs since 2012**.

**Social inflation** refers to the excessive rise in commercial insurers' costs due to "nuclear verdicts" — jury awards that often start at a million dollars and grow exponentially.

Aaron Black, Director of Business Development for Keystone Insurers Group, notes: *"Carriers are coming to us as they are seeing a 25 to 30 percent increase in commercial insurance premiums across the board."*

Carriers deemed especially risky — those with poor **Compliance, Safety, and Accountability (CSA) scores** — will see even bigger premium increases as underwriters scrutinize safety and compliance ever more carefully.

---

## Not All Data Tells a Complete Story

Most underwriters use CSA data to guide their decisions. The problem? If the carrier has implemented specific risk mitigation protocols to lower their safety scores, the results of these efforts won't necessarily be reflected in the report.

Ryan Smith, president of Kingsgate Insurance in Iowa, summarizes the issue: *"They'll pull that report, and they'll get back to you and maybe issue a decision before you even get them an ounce of actual specific information about the insured."*

Refuting CSA data without tangible evidence is a hard sell. It's up to the carrier and the agent to be proactive by providing underwriters with the complete story **before** the underwriting process begins.

Smith adds: *"Underwriters want to see an extended period of time of you performing without losses or negligible losses before they really discount some of that past loss history... So you've got to be able to present something that is tangible."*

---

## Why Improving Your Risk Profile is Critical

Michael Kuiros, Director of DOT & Fleet Services and Risk Management for Keystone, says: *"A lot of things are left out of your control. The things that are left in your control would be your CSA score."*

CSA scores range from **0 to 100**, with higher numbers indicating increased risk due to accidents, DOT violations, failed roadside inspections, or a combination of these factors.

> *"This is a big section of what an underwriter would be looking at when they are underwriting your company as far as taking you on as a risk."* — Michael Kuiros

**What you do behind the scenes to lower your CSA score, build a safety culture, and quantify these efforts to underwriters is critical.**

---

## Technology Innovation Provides a Crucial Link

Technology now exists so that fleet owners and safety managers can better monitor driver behavior and CSA scores — effectively shifting from a reactive environment to a proactive one.

Scott Mogensen, Head of Solutions Enablement at Foley, says: *"It's not a matter of looking at a report every now and again... but actually having a process with rigor that's digital that allows a safety manager to be empowered to see what's going on and react to situations that are brought to them through daily alerts."*

### What Underwriters Want to See:
- Installing **telematics** (like cab cameras) so driver behavior can be monitored and scored
- Monitoring violations, tickets, accidents, and other incidents that adversely affect your risk profile
- Evidence that you address issues **immediately**, not just at insurance renewal

---

## Monitoring Success: The Data

Foley analyzed 13 transportation companies using Foley's platform and monitoring and compared their BASICs to 13 companies that don't use Foley's solution.

**Transportation companies using Foley's solution had lower scores in four out of five BASIC categories.**

The FMCSA's seven BASICs used to determine a motor carrier's safety performance:
1. Vehicle Maintenance
2. Unsafe Driving
3. Crash Indicator
4. Hazardous Materials (HM)
5. Driver Fitness
6. Controlled Substance/Alcohol
7. Hours-of-Service (HOS)

The Foley programs analyzed included:
- Background checks
- MVR monitoring
- Drug and alcohol testing
- DOT Clearinghouse
- DOT physical management

Sample companies represented industries including Energy, Utilities, Waste, Construction, Transportation, Retail, Manufacturing, and Consumer Services.

### What This Data Means for the Underwriting Process

Transportation companies using Foley's solution have tangible data they can share with underwriters — data that highlights ongoing efforts to improve their risk profile and how those efforts are paying off.

Joel Sitak, CEO of Foley Services, explains: *"The agent can really show the underwriter that their client is serious about reducing risk... It's getting access to that data faster so that you can take action on it before it becomes a problem in the underwriting process."*

These proactive measures can yield meaningful cost benefits: *"You'll ultimately get a lower premium or not have premiums go up as high as they might in other circumstances because we can prove that we're going to monitor drivers, and we're going to take action on drivers when bad things happen."*

## Foley's CSA Monitor: What You Need to Know

Foley's CSA Monitor sends a **daily email** alerting you to issues with your company's CSA score in real time.

### Key Benefits:

**Get alerted quickly.** When a CSA infraction registers with the FMCSA, you'll get notified ASAP — not six months later at your insurance renewal.

**Fix mistakes sooner.** Incorrect reporting does happen. Daily emails let you dispute errors before they reach the underwriter and affect your premiums.

**Foster a culture of safety.** Monitored drivers behave better. Sitak notes: *"When employees know they're being monitored, they behave better. Commercial drivers have a lower positive drug test rate than non-drivers because they know they're going to be randomly tested."*

**Have critical evidence for negotiating with underwriters.** You can prove you're always working on reducing risk and addressing issues quickly — with hard data to back it up.

---

## Bottom Line: Hard Data Drives Decisions in Hard Markets

Underwriting is a storytelling process. With the right data, carriers and agents can tell a complete story that gives the underwriter confidence in the carrier — resulting in better premiums.

Tim Weaverling from RWR summarizes: *"We try to go in with a claims analysis, a driver profile, and as much information as we can on why those scores look good or bad, and just to get them to the table."*
