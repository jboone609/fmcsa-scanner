## The State of the Commercial Auto Insurance Market

Under today's market conditions, high-risk industries like heavy fleets and transportation — and those with lower primary coverage limits — are seeing **higher rate increases**.

More claims are now reaching the umbrella layer, especially in high-risk areas like construction, heavy fleet/transportation, and healthcare. This has led to stricter underwriting rules and ongoing premium hikes.

Over the past decade, the risks and potential liability from commercial trucks have been a major topic of discussion. Factors driving change include:

- **Distracted driving**
- **Rising legal costs**
- **Large jury awards**, partly driven by third-party funding for lawsuits

These factors have significantly changed how underwriters view excess liability coverage for commercial fleets.

### A Growing Concern: Contingent Third-Party Auto Hauling

A new topic of concern has emerged regarding the liability insureds face from hiring third parties to haul their property and products. Numerous cases of neglect from both hiring companies and actual freight haulers have led to **nuclear verdicts** — litigations often resulting in multi-million-dollar settlements.

*Source: WTW Insurance Marketplace Realities 2025 Report*

## Trending Trucking Risks & Real-Life Nuclear Verdicts

The commercial insurance sector is seeing a growing number of large settlements and verdicts, often **exceeding $20 million**. The shortage of truck drivers has led some companies to lower their hiring standards, which has worsened their loss history.

### Recent Nuclear Verdict Cases

- A company hired a logistics company to transport its product. The driver was involved in an accident resulting in severe injuries to a child and the death of a mother. **The plaintiff sued the hiring company for negligent hiring of the logistics company.**

- A company hired a third-party transporter that subcontracted the job to another driver who caused an accident killing an elderly couple. **The company was deemed to have granted "implicit" permission for the subcontracting.**

- A contract driver's medical condition resulted in an accident involving multiple fatalities and a severely injured child. The hiring company had not investigated the carrier's drivers. **The court ruled that the allegations of negligent hiring should be decided by a jury.**

*Source: Chubb Insurance Co., 3rd Party Hauling White Paper*

## The Connection Between CSA Scores & Insurance Premiums

Most insurance underwriters use CAB Reports to determine their pricing decisions. But if a company has implemented specific risk mitigation protocols to lower their CSA score, the results of those efforts won't be reflected in the report unless they can show the underwriter hard data to back up their claims.

**Your company's CSA score can influence your insurance premiums — for better or for worse. High scores can mean high premiums; low or zero scores mean more appealing premiums.**

> *"Most insurance companies will deny coverage simply based on a high CSA score, and then you end up with a high-risk insurance carrier. Those carriers won't look at your losses at all — especially if you have less than 10 drivers. Then what they are looking at is each driver's MVR and CSA scores. Nothing else."*
>
> — **Michael B. Kuiros, CWCA**, Director of DOT & Fleet Services | Risk Management, Keystone

### Using Technology to Tell the Safety Story

Fleet owners and safety managers can now better monitor driver behavior and CSA scores, shifting from a reactive environment to a more proactive one — which is the exact type of safety environment underwriters want to see.

Recommendations:

- **Install telematics**, like cameras inside the cab, to monitor driver behavior.
- **Monitor the results** of poor driving, poor maintenance, or poor paperwork — violations, tickets, accidents — take corrective action and document those actions to present to underwriters.

## How DOT Violations Impact CSA Scores

Any DOT violation received by your drivers or company remains on your record for **two years**. If your CSA score becomes too high, the FMCSA may decide to give you a compliance review. If your score suggests you are a danger on the road, an out-of-service order may be issued.

**Having a CSA score monitoring solution in place can help avoid surprise traffic or safety violations being revealed during DOT audits or the insurance underwriting process.**

## How to Improve Your CSA Score & Reduce Risks

### Hire Drivers Carefully
Balance getting trucks on the road with making sure your drivers don't have red flags. Run background checks before hiring.

### Train Drivers Properly & Regularly
Make sure your drivers understand how to handle difficult situations. Review incidents regularly and provide safety reminders to keep them top of mind.

### Maintain Company Vehicles
Drivers should report vehicle issues requiring repairs immediately. Open communication on mechanical and safety issues allows everyone to work toward a better CSA score.

### Complete Pre-Trip Checks
Remind drivers to check vehicles before leaving. This can help ensure they and the company aren't cited for violations that could have been easily prevented.

## How MVR Checks Reduce Your Risk Profile

As a DOT-regulated business, you're required to pull **annual MVRs** for every driver you employ. But what happens when a driver gets a DUI or commits a traffic violation after you pull their annual report?

**Running regular MVR checks on your drivers can help avoid surprise violations being revealed by insurance underwriters.**

### How Does MVR Monitoring Work?

**MVR Monitoring** tracks CDL drivers' license records on an ongoing basis. When there is a change, employers are immediately alerted to any "hit" and receive the updated MVR for review.

"Hits" can include:

- Accidents, DUIs, speeding tickets, or other violations/convictions
- CDL suspensions or revocations
- Safety violations
- Medical certificate downgrades
- Positive changes such as CDL renewals or medical certificate updates

This is the proof you need to show underwriters you're taking proactive measures to operate a safe company.
