# Glossary of Terms and Abbreviations
*Last updated by Lee Boswell | May 13, 2024 at 10:53 AM EDT*

---

| Term | Description | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Adverse Action** | Handling of information found during the screening process that might prevent a driver from being hired. | Ability to give candidates and employees the opportunity to review the accuracy of the information contained in the report so that they can request corrections if inaccuracies are found. https://www.foleyservices.com/news/hiring-drivers-what-you-need-to-know-about-adverse-action/ |
| **ATS** | Applicant Tracking System | System to connect recruiters with job seekers and manage the hiring process. Competitor products include TenStreet, DriverReach. |
| **BASIC Percentile** | Behavioral Analysis and Safety Improvement Category Percentile | FMCSA's CSA program uses SMS to analyze violations, inspections, crashes to assign percentile to carriers. Higher the percentile, worse the performance. |
| **BAT** | Breath Alcohol Technician | A technician qualified in DOT breath alcohol testing as required by 49 CFR Part 40. |
| **BGC** | Background Check | Used for hiring decisions, regulated by FCRA and FTC. |
| **BOC-3 Filing** | Blanket of Coverage Filing ("Designation of Agents for Service of Process") | A federal filing in the US that assigns a process agent to accept legal documents on behalf of a transportation or logistics company in each state the company is authorized to do business. |
| **CAP** | Corrective Action Plan | The CAP must be submitted to FMCSA within 15 days from the date of written notice that the new entrant failed the safety audit. |
| **CCF** | Custody and Control Form | A form used to record drug testing data. For DOT DAT, there is a specific Federal Drug Testing CCF that has a section for MRO input. |
| **CDL** | Commercial Driver's License — a driver's license required to operate large, heavy or placarded hazardous material vehicles in the US. | There are a few different types of CMVs that require a driver to hold a valid CDL. The driver may be required to obtain one or more special endorsements, such as Air Brakes, Hazmat, Tankers or Passenger Vehicles, in order to drive a truck with double or triple trailers, a truck with a tank, a truck carrying hazardous materials, or a passenger vehicle. |
| **CFPB** | Consumer Financial Protection Bureau | Enforce FCRA and oversee how credit information is collected and used. |
| **CFR** | Code of Federal Regulations | https://www.govinfo.gov/app/collection/cfr/ |
| **CH** | Drug and Alcohol Clearinghouse | https://clearinghouse.fmcsa.dot.gov/ — Mandatory for drivers and employers to register at Clearinghouse to report violations and run queries. |
| **CMV** | Commercial Motor Vehicles — can be subject to FMCSA regulations. | Subject to FMCSA regulations if any of the following types of CMV in interstate commerce: a vehicle with a GVWR or GCWR (whichever is greater) of 4,537 kg (10,001 lbs.) or more; a vehicle designed or used to transport between 9 and 15 passengers (including the driver) for compensation, whether direct or indirect; a vehicle designed or used to transport 15 or more passengers including the driver and not used for compensation; any size vehicle used in the transportation of materials found to be hazardous under the Hazardous Materials Transportation Act. |
| **CSA** | Compliance Safety Accountability | FMCSA's safety, compliance and enforcement program. https://csa.fmcsa.dot.gov |
| **CSL** | Collection Site Locator | — |
| **CVSA** | Commercial Vehicle Safety Alliance — https://www.cvsa.org/ | A nonprofit association comprised of local, state, provincial, territorial and federal CMV safety officials and industry representatives. Key program is the annual International Roadcheck. CVSA certified officials conduct roadside inspections aimed to focus on certain compliance areas and serve as educational opportunities. |
| **DAPM** | Drug and Alcohol Program Manager | Key person at an employer who is responsible for overall management of drug and alcohol program. |
| **DAT** | Drug and Alcohol Testing | See notes for DATCOM. |
| **DATCOM** | DAT Compliance (Foley DAT program manual) | FMCSA and DOT require that persons subject to the CDL requirements and their employers follow alcohol and drug testing rules. These rules include procedures for testing, frequency of tests, and substances tested for. |
| **DER** | Designated Employer Representative | The contact person at the employer responsible for performing many tasks to co-ordinate and execute the DOT requirements. |
| **DFW** | Drug Free Workplace — policies governed by various federal agencies | There is no requirement for most private employers to have a drug-free workplace policy of any kind. The exceptions to this are federal contractors and grantees, as well as safety- and security-sensitive industries and positions. |
| **DOE** | Department of Energy | — |
| **DOT** | Department of Transportation | — |
| **DQF** | Driver Qualification File | — |
| **DRS** | Document Review System | — |
| **DVIR** | Driver Vehicle Inspection Report | A formal record confirming that a driver has completed an inspection on a commercial motor vehicle stating that it is safe for operation. Inspections are carried out at the beginning and end of the day (pre-trip and post-trip inspection). Regulated by FMCSA Title 49 Section 396.11. |
| **ELD** | Electronic Logging Device | A device that is integrated with vehicle engine to automatically record driving time. FMCSA mandates the use of ELD by drivers required to report HOS. |
| **EPN** | Employer Pull Notice | EPN enables organizations to monitor driving records of employees who drive for the company, by registering with the state motor vehicle department. This is state specific; some may be mandatory. |
| **FAA** | Federal Aviation Authority | Agency under DOT responsible for aviation management and safety. |
| **FCR** | Fair Credit Reporting | See FCRA. |
| **FCRA** | Fair Credit Reporting Act | A federal law that oversees credit reporting agencies in order to ensure all consumer credit information is fair and accurate. The FCRA details what data can be collected, who can view a credit report and under what provisions. |
| **FHWA** | Federal Highway Administration | https://highways.dot.gov/ — Division under DOT responsible for highway and motor vehicle safety. |
| **FMCSA** | Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration | Division under DOT responsible for safety of commercial motor vehicles. https://www.fmcsa.dot.gov/ |
| **FRA** | Federal Railroad Administration | https://dotcms.fra.dot.gov/ — Division under DOT responsible for safety of railroads. |
| **FTA** | Federal Transit Administration | FTA |
| **FTC** | Federal Trade Commission | Responsible for protecting consumers from fraud, identity theft, scams. |
| **GFW** | Gross Vehicle Weight | Total weight of a truck and payload at any given time. |
| **GVWR** | Total weight of a truck and payload at any given time | Maximum weight rating established by the chassis manufacturer. |
| **HM** | Hazardous Materials | — |
| **HOS** | Hours of Service | — |
| **IFTA** | International Fuel Tax Agreement | IFTA is an agreement between the lower 48 states of the United States and the Canadian provinces, to simplify the reporting of fuel use by motor carriers that operate in more than one jurisdiction. |
| **IRP** | International Registration Plan | IRP is a reciprocity agreement among states of the US, the District of Columbia and provinces of Canada which recognizes the registration of commercial motor vehicles registered by other jurisdictions. |
| **ISP** | Initial Screening Pak | Foley application form that contains all information listed in Title 49 CFR 391.21. |
| **LCV** | Longer Combination Vehicle | Tractor-trailer combinations with two or more trailers that may exceed 80,000 pounds GVW. |
| **MC#** | Motor Carrier Number | Carriers that transport passengers or regulated commodities in interstate commerce need this "authority to operate" above and beyond the DOT #. |
| **MCMIS** | Motor Carrier Management Information System | Contains information on the safety fitness of commercial motor carriers and hazardous material shippers subject to the FMCSR and the Hazardous Materials Regulations. |
| **MCS 150** | Form used to update DOT number | Update filed once every 24 months to ensure info related to DOT# is current. For carriers out of business, if they have not informed DOT, they still need to file this. |
| **MRO** | Medical Review Officer | A licensed physician responsible for receiving and reviewing laboratory results generated by an employer's drug testing program and evaluating medical explanations for certain drug test results. |
| **MVR** | Motor Vehicle Record | A public record of a license holder's driving history. |
| **MVR-M** | Motor Vehicle Record Monitoring | A program that detects changes to a MVR through continuous monitoring, enabling identifying any serious issues in a timely manner. |
| **NHTSA** | National Highway Transit Safety Administration | Federal agency under DOT responsible for road and vehicle safety. |
| **NRC** | Nuclear Regulatory Commission | — |
| **OO** | Owner / Operator | A small business owner who also is an operator (DOT driver in Foley context). |
| **OOS** | Out of Service | A driver or a CMV can be designated OOS during an inspection if certain violations are uncovered. An OOS driver or vehicle cannot return to the road until the violation is corrected. |
| **PA PE RS** | DAT Testing Types | PA - Post Accident, PE - Pre-Employment, RS - Random Screen. |
| **PA** | Post Accident | — |
| **PE** | Pre-Employment | — |
| **Phase 0** | — | Refers to leads generated by scraping DOT websites to find new entrants that are in the middle of a DOT# application. |
| **PHMSA** | Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration | — |
| **PSP** | Pre-employment Screening Program | Provides access to driver's 5 years of crash and 3 years of inspection history from FMCSA MCMIS. |
| **PWA** | — | — |
| **RGU** | Revenue Generating Unit | — |
| **RS** | Random Screening | — |
| **RTD** | Return to Duty | — |
| **SAFER** | Safety and Fitness Electronic Records System | Provides access to a company's safety data over internet (search by DOT#, MC# or name). Safety Audit. |
| **Safety Sensitive** | DOT classification for jobs where the employee can impact the safety of themselves or others | Examples include: Pipeline workers, flight crew and attendants, drivers of >16 passengers, and hazardous material drivers. |
| **SAMHSA** | Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration | — |
| **SAP** | Substance Abuse Professional | — |
| **SMS** | Safety Measurement System | Tool used by FMCSA to identify carriers with potential safety problems. https://csa.fmcsa.dot.gov/Documents/SMSMethodology.pdf |
| **SPE** | Skills Performance Evaluation | — |
| **SPH** | Skills Performance History | — |
| **SSI** | Safety Sensitive Individual | An individual employed in a safety sensitive job. |
| **TAT** | Turn Around Time | — |
| **TPA** | Third Party Administrator | An organization that processes insurance claims, regulatory documents, or certain aspects of employee benefit plans for a separate entity. |
| **UCR** | Unified Carrier Registrations | — |
| **USCG** | United States Coast Guard | Created by the Unified Carrier Registration Act of 2005 (UCR Act - 49 United States Code (USC) section 14504a); it replaces the former system for registering and collecting fees from operators of vehicles engaged in interstate travel — the Single State Registration System (SSRS). |
