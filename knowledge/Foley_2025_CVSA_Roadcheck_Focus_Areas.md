## Hours-of-Service Compliance

Hours-of-service regulations limit driving hours and mandate rest breaks to prevent commercial drivers from operating vehicles while fatigued. But the regulations are only effective if drivers accurately reflect their times and duty statuses in their Records of Duty Status (RODS).

When inspectors review a driver's RODS for compliance, they will also check for:

- Electronic logging device tampering (driving while not logged in, disconnected devices, improper edits)
- Ghost drivers (claiming a co-driver when there is no co-driver present)
- Improper use of personal conveyance
- Improper use of exceptions, like adverse driving conditions
- Recording off-duty time while working

---

## Tire Maintenance & Repairs

Proper tire maintenance is crucial to any company's safety and success. A flat tire — or worse, a blowout — on the road poses a serious risk to all drivers and can lead to significant delays.

During tire inspections, inspectors will look for:

- Low tread depth
- Audible air leaks
- Flat tires
- Belt material or casing ply exposed in the tread or sidewall
- Tread or sidewall separation
- Bulges in the sidewall
- Improper repairs in the sidewall
- Items lodged between dual tires

---

## Tips for Safety Managers & Owner-Operators

- Ensure all CDLs/CLPs and medical certificates are up to date and in drivers' possessions
- Encourage drivers to have records of duty status (RODS) and supporting documents ready
- Print any driver exemptions currently in use to provide to inspectors

---

## Tips for Drivers

- Keep updated CDLs/CLPs, medical certificates, and skill performance evaluation certificates with you
- Wear fastened seatbelts at all times
- If you operate an electronic logging device (ELD), know how to use it and be able to perform the tasks requested by inspectors
