## Check Your DOT Compliance To-Do List

The end of each year seems to sneak up on everyone, but that doesn't mean you can let your annual compliance requirements slip away. If your business is regulated by the Department of Transportation (DOT), you must check all the boxes when it comes to your yearly compliance matters, including:

- Motor Vehicle Report (MVR) Checks
- Unified Carrier Registration (UCR)
- FMCSA Clearinghouse Queries

Failing to meet any of these requirements can result in hefty fines.

## 1. Annual Motor Vehicle Report (MVR)

Every year you must run an updated motor vehicle report (MVR) on your drivers to ensure they are qualified to continue driving a commercial motor vehicle (CMV) — and that there are no safety issues requiring attention. The report looks into a driver's current license status, accident history, and record of driving violations.

This new motor vehicle report should be placed in your employee's driver qualification file to replace last year's MVR.

**What happens if that MVR check comes back with a violation?** As an employer, it's up to you to determine the severity of the situation and take the appropriate corrective action — providing a written warning, suspending the employee, or even terminating them.

### MVR Monitoring

The annual MVR check is a justifiable requirement, but it doesn't let you see driver violations in real time. After running the check, you could be in the dark for months before finding out about potentially dangerous traffic violations. That's where MVR monitoring comes in.

MVR monitoring closes the gap between annual checks and undetected violations. MVR monitoring can also help **reduce insurance premiums** by demonstrating proactive fleet safety management.

> **Compliance Requirement:** Run a new MVR check on each employee at least once during a 12-month period.

## 2. Annual Unified Carrier Registration (UCR)

UCR stands for Unified Carrier Registration program — a federally mandated system for registering operators of commercial motor vehicles (CMVs) in North America.

Whether you operate CMVs in two states or 20, you are required to complete your UCR filing every year, as long as you meet the requirements below.

### Who Must File?

Any motor carrier who drives a commercial vehicle carrying cargo over state or international lines must file an annual UCR. Brokers, freight forwarders, and leasing companies are also subject to the UCR fee.

Specifically, any business that operates CMVs meeting the following thresholds is required to file:

- Has a gross vehicle weight (GVW) over 10,000 pounds, or
- Carries placarded amounts of hazardous materials, or
- Transports more than 10 passengers (including driver)

### Consequences of Non-Compliance

If you don't pay for the UCR and are caught driving over state lines, enforcement officials could detain your vehicles. Fines range from **$100 to $5,000** for first-time offenders depending on the state.

### Non-Participating States

Currently, 41 states participate in the UCR program. The following do **not** participate:

1. Arizona
2. Florida
3. Hawaii
4. Maryland
5. Nevada
6. New Jersey
7. Oregon
8. Vermont
9. Wyoming
10. District of Columbia

> **Important:** If your state doesn't participate, you are still required to purchase a UCR filing in the nearest participating state.

> **Compliance Requirement:** File your Unified Carrier Registration for the following calendar year on an annual basis.

## 3. Annual FMCSA Clearinghouse Queries

As a motor carrier, you're required to run a Clearinghouse query on all your DOT-regulated drivers every year. Limited queries fulfill the annual requirement.

### Full vs. Limited Query

**Limited Query** — Reveals if a driver's record exists in the Clearinghouse, but specific violation information is not released. Paper or electronic general driver consent is required.

**Full Query** — Provides detailed information about any drug and alcohol program violations in a driver's Clearinghouse record. Electronic driver consent must be given in the Clearinghouse beforehand.

> **Important Note:** The annual query does not meet the pre-employment query requirement, since the annual is a limited query that only shows if a driver's information exists in the Clearinghouse.

### Clearinghouse Violations

If a limited query returns violations, you'll need to run a **full Clearinghouse query within 24 hours**. If you can't get electronic driver consent in that time, the driver won't be able to perform safety-sensitive functions until you do.

You must keep a record of all FMCSA Clearinghouse queries and responses for **three years**. As of January 6, 2023, maintaining a valid Clearinghouse registration fulfills this DOT recordkeeping requirement.

> **Compliance Requirement:** After receiving consent, perform a limited query in the FMCSA Clearinghouse on each of your drivers at least once per year.

## 4. 2023 FMCSA Clearinghouse & Safety Performance History Updates

As of **January 6, 2023** — three years since the Clearinghouse was established — DOT-regulated companies no longer need to manually check a new driver's Safety Performance History with previous employers for drug-and-alcohol-related violation information. Simply conducting a **pre-employment query in the Clearinghouse** now fulfills that requirement.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I still need to contact a driver's previous employers for other information?**

Yes. You still need to contact former FMCSA-regulated employers from the past three years to confirm:

- Employment verification (dates, vehicles operated, etc.)
- FMCSA-reported crashes — severe crashes within the past three years meeting FMCSA thresholds

**Do I still need to run annual FMCSA Clearinghouse queries?**

Yes. The annual query requirement is not affected by this update. CDL driver employers must conduct a limited query at least once per year on a rolling 12-month basis.

**What if my employee worked in another federally regulated industry?**

If the employee worked for an employer regulated by a DOT agency other than FMCSA (e.g., Federal Railroad Administration or Federal Aviation Administration) and was subject to DOT drug or alcohol screens, you must still contact those employers and ask about:

- Alcohol tests with a 0.04 or higher alcohol concentration result
- Verified positive DOT drug screen results
- Testing refusals
- Other violations of DOT drug and alcohol testing regulations

If any violations are found, you need documentation showing the employee completed DOT return-to-duty requirements.
